## 1) Board Resolution (for adoption)

**Resolution: Delegation of Financial and Contracting Authority**

**WHEREAS**, the Board of Directors ("Board") of Disagree Wisely, Inc. is responsible for financial oversight and may delegate certain authority to officers and agents;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED** that, effective September 22, 2025, the Board delegates authority as follows, subject to this Resolution:

### General Delegation & Thresholds

The Board delegates spending authority as follows:

**1. Pre-Approved Recurring Expenses** (see "Standing/Recurring Expenses" section below)
- These categories have their own monthly caps and do not count against other spending limits
- Employee payroll (for existing employees) is excluded from all spending caps

**2. All Other Expenses** (not in pre-approved categories)
- The President and Treasurer, acting jointly, may approve expenses up to $10,000 per transaction and $30,000 monthly limit
- Either the President or Treasurer, acting alone, may approve expenses up to $500 per transaction and $3,000 monthly limit

### Exception for Major Thresholds

The dollar thresholds in "Items Requiring Prior Board Approval" do not apply to expenses within the pre-approved recurring categories below (except for new debt, related-party transactions, and non-cancelable contracts exceeding 12 months or $10,000 annual auto-renewal value).

### Standing/Recurring Expenses (Pre-Approved)

The following expenses are hereby pre-approved for ongoing operations without additional authorization:

- **Development Services**: Development work with existing contractors (those previously approved by the President and Treasurer) for CEO/CTOO (Chief Technology and Operations Officer)-assigned tasks.
  - *Monthly cap: $30,000*
- **Platform Operations**: AI services (OpenAI, Anthropic); core infrastructure (Google Cloud, Sentry, SendGrid, Twilio, Heroku).
  - *Monthly cap: $10,000*
- **News/Media Subscriptions**: Publications relevant to organizational mission (e.g., Blocked and Reported, Persuasion, etc.).
  - *Monthly cap: $500 combined*
- **Office Supplies**: Under $200 per transaction.
  - *Monthly cap: $1,000*
- **Employee Payroll**: Payroll for existing employees through approved payroll services (e.g., Gusto).
  - *No monthly cap - excluded from all spending limits*

### Expenses Allowing President and Treasurer Approval (Delegated Authority)

The President and Treasurer, acting jointly, may approve expenses not in the pre-approved categories above, up to $10,000 per transaction and $30,000 monthly limit, including:

- **Travel**: Business travel expenses (airfare, lodging, ground transportation, meals). International travel requires pre-trip approval.
- **Office/Facilities**: Furniture, equipment, supplies over $200, assembly services.
- **Personnel**: Payroll service fees; recruiting tools (LinkedIn).
- **Finance & Compliance**: Bookkeeping services; compliance filings; trademark fees.
- **Security & Operations**: Security audits; payment processing (e.g., Bill.com); operational subscriptions.
- **Marketing**: Website tools; advertising; agent/podcast booking services.
- **New Development Contractors**: Within approved limits.

**Legal Services and Insurance (Special Limits):**

- **Legal Services**: Up to $10,000 per transaction, $20,000 monthly limit. **All legal expenses require written communication** (email thread or approval in finance system tied to invoice/receipt).
- **Insurance**: New policies or major coverage changes, up to $5,000 per transaction, $10,000 monthly limit. **Requires written communication** (not routine renewals).

### Expenses Allowing President or Treasurer Solo Approval

Either the President or Treasurer, acting alone, may approve expenses in the same categories as joint approval above, but limited to $500 per transaction and $3,000 monthly limit.

### Items Requiring Prior Board Approval (Not Delegated)

All expenses and commitments not covered by the delegated authority above require prior Board approval, including but not limited to:

- Any single commitment exceeding $25,000 or any multi-year obligation with a total value over $50,000.
- New debt, credit lines, collateralized obligations, or capital purchases over $10,000.
- Grants or subgrants to third parties over $15,000 per recipient per fiscal year.
- New employees (full-time or part-time).
- New or amended executive employment agreements, severance packages, or compensation plans.
- Related-party transactions or any transaction presenting a conflict of interest under the Board-adopted conflict of interest policy.
- Any other significant financial commitment or policy change not explicitly delegated herein.

### Contracts & Term Limits

The President and Treasurer may sign vendor contracts up to 12-month terms (with standard renewals) within their spending authority. Multi-year or auto-renew contracts that exceed $10,000 annually require Board approval unless cancelable without penalty on ≤ 30 days' notice.

### Documentation, Reporting & Oversight

- All payments require supporting documentation (invoices, receipts, contracts).
- Purpose/business justification must be documented for each expense.
- Maintain vendor files including W-9 (US vendors), insurance certificates where applicable, and conflict of interest disclosures if any.
- The Treasurer will provide the Board a quarterly statement of activity, cash balance, and a summary of expenses by category.
- All delegated authority remains subject to the bylaws and state law; the Board may amend or revoke this delegation at any time.

### Conflicts of Interest

Comply with the Board-adopted conflict of interest policy; conflicted individuals must recuse from approval and negotiation.

### Emergency Authority

In bona fide emergencies affecting safety or critical continuity:
- Either the President or Treasurer, acting alone, may approve up to $10,000
- The President and Treasurer, acting jointly, may approve up to $25,000
- Emergency expenditures require Board notification within 24 hours and ratification at the next Board meeting

### Annual Review

This Resolution and spending authority shall be reviewed at least annually and whenever thresholds are exceeded or business model changes.

### Ratification of Prior Expenditures

The Board ratifies expenditures listed in Exhibit A that are consistent with this Resolution.

**APPROVED by the Board of Directors on September 22, 2025.**
