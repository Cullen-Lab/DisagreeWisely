## **DEFENSE COSTS - Why This Matters**

Imagine you get sued and the claim is covered by your insurance:

**Defense costs OUTSIDE the limit (what you thought you were getting):**
- You have $1M for damages/settlements
- Legal fees are paid separately and unlimited
- Example: If legal fees are $400K and settlement is $800K, insurance pays all $1.2M

**Defense costs INSIDE the limit (what the CFC policy actually shows):**
- You have $1M total for EVERYTHING (legal fees + damages combined)
- Legal fees eat into your coverage
- Example: If legal fees are $400K and settlement is $800K, you only have $600K left for settlement. You'd have to pay $200K out of pocket.

**Why this is a big deal for you:**
- D&O claims against nonprofits can involve expensive legal defense (employment disputes, board decisions, etc.)
- Cyber/E&O claims can be very technical and require expensive expert witnesses
- If defense costs are inside the limit, a complex lawsuit could use up your coverage before you even pay damages
- This could mean hundreds of thousands out of pocket

**The contradiction:** The D&O quote says "outside" but the general terms say "inside." The CFC policy appears to have costs "inside" but you thought both would be "outside."

## **RETROACTIVE DATES - Why This Matters**

**What is a retroactive date?**
These are "claims made" policies, meaning:
- A claim must be MADE during the policy period (Oct 2025 - Oct 2026)
- BUT it can be for something that HAPPENED earlier (going back to the retroactive date)

**Example:**
- Retroactive date = June 1, 2024
- Policy period = Oct 2025 - Oct 2026
- Something bad happened in July 2024 during your pilot
- Someone sues you in March 2026
- ✅ COVERED (happened after retroactive date, sued during policy period)

**If retroactive date = "Inception" (10/7/2025):**
- Something bad happened in July 2024 during your pilot
- Someone sues you in March 2026
- ❌ NOT COVERED (happened before the retroactive date)

**Why this is critical:**
- You operated Sway from June 2024 - present
- If the retroactive date is October 2025, you have ZERO coverage for the first 16 months of operations
- Any issues from your pilot program, early students, early funding conversations - none of it would be covered
- This defeats the entire purpose of getting "full prior acts" coverage

## **TERRORISM COVERAGE - Why This Matters**

This is actually straightforward - terrorism coverage is included for free ($0 additional premium). You just need to confirm you're accepting it (not rejecting it).

Under TRIA (Terrorism Risk Insurance Act), the government helps reimburse insurers for certified acts of terrorism, so they can offer it cheaply.

**Why you want it:** If a terrorist attack somehow affected your operations or led to a claim, you'd be covered.
