Scientific Research Organizer — Disagree Wisely, Inc.


1. Describe in detail the research project(s) planned

Our research program investigates how to foster constructive disagreement, reduce polarization, and improve reasoning and communication skills through educational interventions. We focus on understanding the effects of structured cross-perspective dialogue — including dialogue facilitated by AI — on these outcomes. Our research activities fall into three main areas:

(a) Longitudinal classroom studies. We will study how students' attitudes, dispositions, and communication skills develop over the course of a semester when they regularly engage in structured, text-based discussions with peers who hold different viewpoints. Using de-identified data from student volunteers at partner universities, we aim to replicate and extend preliminary findings suggesting that structured cross-perspective dialogue can substantially reduce student self-censorship, increase willingness to engage with challenging ideas, and improve measurable argumentation and communication skills. Because students will participate in discussions at regular intervals throughout a semester, we can track the development of these skills over time using pre/post assessments, standardized instruments, and behavioral measures.

(b) Large-scale controlled experiments on AI-facilitated dialogue. We are planning empirical studies involving approximately 1,000 American adults recruited via established research platforms (e.g., Prolific Academic). These studies will rigorously test whether AI-facilitated text-based dialogue can reduce polarization and promote mutual understanding between people with opposing ideological views. In controlled experimental designs, we will compare different facilitation approaches — varying, for example, the level of proactive guidance provided by an AI facilitator, the degree of emotional sensitivity in AI responses, and the types of prompts and scaffolding offered. We will examine how the effectiveness of these approaches depends on discussion topic, participant demographics, strength of opinions, and the dynamics of conversations as they unfold.

(c) Open science and data sharing. We will make our de-identified conversational datasets publicly available through online repositories (e.g., Open Science Framework, Dataverse) for use by researchers across disciplines — including social psychology, human-computer interaction, conflict studies, political science, and communication. These datasets, which will capture rich information about how people discuss contentious topics, will be valuable far beyond our own research questions. We aim to help define and advance the emerging multidisciplinary field of AI-scaffolded human communication.

Our research will employ a variety of educational discussion tools — not exclusively any single platform — and we will collaborate with researchers at other academic and nonprofit institutions. All research findings will be published in peer-reviewed journals and made publicly available.

3. How is the research being conducted going to be made publicly accessible?

Through publications in peer-reviewed scientific journals, open-access preprint servers (e.g., SSRN), online data repositories (e.g., Open Science Framework, Dataverse), and on our organization's website (www.disagreewisely.org).


4. How will the organization ensure that they will not have any contracts or sponsored research with any individual or for-profit entity for the benefit of the contracting individual or for-profit entity?

All of our research is designed to advance scientific understanding of constructive disagreement, civil discourse, and educational methods. Our safeguards include: (1) all research findings are published openly and made available to the public simultaneously — no individual or entity receives early or exclusive access to results; (2) our board of directors maintains a conflict of interest policy requiring disclosure and recusal when potential conflicts arise; (3) research projects are selected based on their scientific merit and alignment with our charitable mission, not on the basis of any commercial interest; and (4) before entering into any contract, we will verify that the arrangement is consistent with our exempt purpose and does not primarily benefit any private individual or for-profit entity.


5. Please explain the anticipated plan (or current reality) regarding making various stages of the research public.

We will share interim results and progress updates on our website (www.disagreewisely.org) and deposit preprints on open-access servers (e.g., SSRN) as studies are completed. De-identified datasets will be made available on open repositories (e.g., Open Science Framework) on an ongoing basis. Final results will be submitted for publication in peer-reviewed scientific journals.


7. Is there preliminary research conducted that the organization will use as a base of their current research project?

Yes

Our research builds on prior work by our co-founders in educational settings. In particular, Dr. Simon Cullen conducted preliminary studies using structured cross-perspective discussion formats in university philosophy courses. These studies found that such interventions can halve the number of students who report self-censoring in class, meaningfully increase student willingness to engage with ideas that might be considered offensive or intolerant, and improve self-reported and behavioral measures of communication and argumentation skills. Additionally, Dr. Nicholas DiBella has contributed to the design and evaluation of AI-facilitated discussion approaches in educational contexts. These preliminary findings inform the design and hypotheses of our planned longitudinal classroom studies and controlled experiments.

9. Explain how and by whom research projects are determined and selected.

Research projects are determined by the organization's leadership — currently Dr. Simon Cullen (Chief Executive Officer) and Dr. Nicholas DiBella (Chief Technology and Operations Officer) — based on alignment with our charitable mission and scientific merit. Both hold PhDs and have extensive experience in academic research. Dr. Cullen directs the research agenda, develops study designs and publications, and ensures findings advance understanding of constructive disagreement. Dr. DiBella designs and oversees studies on dialogue effectiveness and civil discourse pedagogy, publishes results, and manages research partnerships. Projects are informed by the existing scientific literature, gaps in current knowledge about constructive disagreement and civil discourse education, and the practical needs of educators and practitioners in the field. As our team grows to include additional research scientists, they will also contribute to shaping the research agenda. The board of directors provides oversight of the overall research program.
