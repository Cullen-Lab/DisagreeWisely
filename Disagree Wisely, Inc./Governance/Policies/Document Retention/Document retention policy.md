### **BOARD RESOLUTION ADOPTING DOCUMENT RETENTION & DESTRUCTION POLICY**

**Disagree Wisely, Inc.**  
Effective Date: November 16, 2025

**WHEREAS**, Disagree Wisely, Inc. (the “Organization”) is required under applicable law and best practices to maintain adequate books and records of its activities, finances, and governance;

**WHEREAS**, the Board of Directors (the “Board”) previously adopted a Financial Authorization Resolution setting expectations for documentation and internal controls;

**WHEREAS**, the Board desires to adopt a formal Document Retention and Destruction Policy to clarify how long various categories of records are to be retained, who is responsible for them, and how they may be destroyed when no longer needed;

**WHEREAS**, the Board intends that this Policy apply to all records of the Organization created since its incorporation on April 2, 2025, including records created before the Organization receives formal recognition of tax-exempt status under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED** that, effective **November 16, 2025**, the Board hereby adopts the **Document Retention and Destruction Policy** attached hereto as **Exhibit A** (the “Policy”);

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the officers of the Organization are authorized and directed to implement this Policy, to communicate it to staff and contractors as appropriate, and to incorporate it into the Organization’s regular financial, governance, and compliance practices;

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that this Policy shall be reviewed periodically by the Treasurer and Secretary (or their successors), and any recommended changes shall be presented to the Board for approval;

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that this resolution and the attached Policy shall be included in the Organization’s permanent corporate records.

## **EXHIBIT A**

### **Disagree Wisely, Inc.**

**Document Retention and Destruction Policy**  
Effective Date: November 16, 2025

#### **1\. Purpose and Scope**

The purpose of this Document Retention and Destruction Policy (“Policy”) is to:

* Ensure that Disagree Wisely, Inc. (the “Organization”) maintains records necessary to meet legal, tax, grant, and governance requirements;  
* Support accurate financial reporting and accountability to funders;  
* Provide clear retention periods for key categories of documents; and  
* Ensure that documents are securely destroyed when they are no longer required.

This Policy applies to **all records** of the Organization, whether created before or after the Effective Date, including records created since the Organization’s incorporation on April 2, 2025, regardless of whether they predate formal recognition of tax-exempt status under Section 501(c)(3).

This Policy works **in conjunction with** the Organization’s Financial Authorization Resolution and any other Board-approved policies.

---

#### **2\. General Principles**

1. **Accuracy and Completeness.** Records should accurately reflect the Organization’s activities and be complete enough to support financial statements, tax filings, and grant reports.  
2. **Minimum Retention.** The retention periods below are **minimums**. Records may be kept longer when necessary (e.g., for grant reporting, audits, or historical purposes).  
3. **Litigation / Investigation Holds.**  
   If the Organization becomes aware of actual or reasonably anticipated litigation, government investigation, audit, or similar proceeding, all routine destruction of records potentially related to the matter must **immediately stop** (a “Litigation Hold”) until counsel or the Board authorizes resumption.  
4. **Confidentiality and Security.** Records containing confidential or personal information must be stored securely and destroyed in a way that protects privacy (e.g., shredding paper, secure deletion of electronic files).  
5. **Format.** Records may be retained in **electronic form** (e.g., PDFs, scans, cloud storage) so long as they are legible, complete, and reasonably accessible.

---

#### **3\. Roles and Responsibilities**

* **Board of Directors**  
  Approves this Policy and any material amendments. Reviews high-level compliance through periodic financial and governance reports.  
* **Secretary**  
  Responsible for maintaining **corporate governance records**, including articles, bylaws, minutes, key resolutions, board policies, and major legal documents.  
* **Treasurer**  
  Responsible for maintaining **financial and accounting records**, including bank and card statements, accounting system records, payroll reports, grant-related financial records, and tax filings. The Treasurer may delegate day-to-day tasks to staff, contractors, or an outside bookkeeper but remains responsible for oversight.  
* **Executive Leadership (President/CEO/CTOO)**  
  Ensures that staff and contractors comply with this Policy in their areas of responsibility and that records are stored in the Organization’s designated systems (e.g., Google Drive, accounting software, payroll provider, payment systems).  
* **All Staff, Officers, and Key Contractors**  
  Must follow this Policy, ensure that records they create/receive for the Organization are stored in the appropriate shared location (not only personal email or devices), and comply with any Litigation Hold instructions.

---

#### **4\. Retention Schedule**

The following retention periods are **minimums**. “Permanent” means the record should be kept indefinitely.

##### **4.1 Corporate Governance & Organizational Records**

| Record Type | Minimum Retention |
| ----- | ----- |
| Articles of Incorporation and amendments | **Permanent** |
| IRS determination letter and 501(c)(3) application (Form 1023/1023-EZ) and supporting materials | **Permanent** |
| Bylaws and amendments | **Permanent** |
| Board and Board committee meeting minutes | **Permanent** |
| Board resolutions and major board policies (including Financial Authorization Resolution, this Policy) | **Permanent** |
| Conflict of interest disclosures and annual acknowledgments | 7 years (longer if related to a specific transaction under review) |
| State corporate registrations and annual reports | **Permanent** |
| Major legal agreements affecting the Organization’s structure or status (e.g., mergers, substantial asset transfers) | **Permanent** |

##### **4.2 Financial, Accounting & Tax Records**

| Record Type | Minimum Retention |
| ----- | ----- |
| Annual financial statements (internal or audited) | **Permanent** |
| General ledger and chart of accounts | **Permanent** |
| Federal and state tax returns (Forms 990/990-EZ/990-N, related schedules, and workpapers) | **Permanent** |
| Sales/use tax filings (if any) | 7 years |
| Bank statements, check images, deposit records | 7 years |
| Credit card statements and supporting documentation | 7 years |
| Canceled checks (if not included with bank statements) | 7 years |
| Vendor invoices, receipts, and bills | 7 years |
| Employee and officer expense reports and supporting receipts | 7 years |
| Payment processor reports (e.g., Bill.com, Stripe, etc.) | 7 years |
| Budget documents and internal financial reports | 7 years |
| Investment account statements and related records | 7 years after disposition of the investment |
| Fixed asset records and depreciation schedules | 7 years after asset is fully depreciated or disposed of |

##### **4.3 Grants, Donations & Program Records**

| Record Type | Minimum Retention |
| ----- | ----- |
| Grant proposals (successful and unsuccessful) | 3 years (unsuccessful); successful proposals follow the grant record retention below |
| Executed grant agreements, amendments, and award letters | At least 7 years after the later of: grant end date **or** submission of final report; longer if required by funder |
| Grant budgets, drawdown/reimbursement requests, and financial reports to funders | At least 7 years after grant end date |
| Programmatic reports and deliverables submitted to funders | At least 7 years after grant end date |
| Donor correspondence and gift agreements for restricted or major gifts | At least 7 years after last related use of funds; **Permanent** for endowment or very long-term restrictions |
| Reimbursable Expense Logs and related backup | 7 years |

##### **4.4 Payroll, Human Resources & Benefits**

*(These apply to employees; for contractors, see “Contracts & Vendors” below.)*

| Record Type | Minimum Retention |
| ----- | ----- |
| Payroll registers and summaries (including Gusto reports) | 7 years |
| Employee earnings records (including W-2 copies) | 7 years |
| Payroll tax returns (Forms 941/940, state equivalents) and deposit records | 7 years |
| Timesheets / time records (if applicable) | 7 years |
| Employee personnel files (application, offer letter, performance reviews, separation documents) | 7 years after termination |
| Employee handbooks and HR policies (final versions) | **Permanent** |
| Benefits plan documents and summaries | **Permanent** |
| I-9 forms | The longer of: 3 years after date of hire **or** 1 year after termination, then destroyed |

##### **4.5 Contracts, Vendors & Insurance**

| Record Type | Minimum Retention |
| ----- | ----- |
| Executed contracts with vendors, consultants, and service providers | 7 years after expiration or termination |
| Supporting procurement documentation (quotes, RFPs, approvals) | 7 years |
| W-9 forms for U.S. vendors and contractors | 7 years after last payment |
| 1099-NEC and 1096 filings and related workpapers | 7 years |
| Insurance policies (general liability, D\&O, workers’ comp, etc.) | 7 years after expiration; **Permanent** for key coverage documentation and summary of historical coverage |
| Insurance claims and related correspondence | 7 years after claim resolved |

##### **4.6 Legal, Compliance & Other Records**

| Record Type | Minimum Retention |
| ----- | ----- |
| Legal opinions and significant legal correspondence | At least 7 years after matter closed; longer if recommended by counsel |
| Records related to complaints, investigations, or disputes | At least 7 years after final resolution; subject to Litigation Hold |
| Regulatory filings and registrations (charitable solicitations, fundraising registrations, etc.) | 7 years; key registrations may be kept permanently |
| Intellectual property registrations (trademarks, copyrights) and related correspondence | **Permanent** |

##### **4.7 Electronic Communications & Working Documents**

* **Emails and messages** that **constitute or support a record category** above (e.g., board approvals, grant negotiations, legal advice, final financial approvals) should be:  
  * Saved to the appropriate shared drive folder, accounting system, or other system of record; and  
  * Retained according to the category they support (e.g., as governance, grant, financial, or legal records).  
* Routine, purely logistical, or transitory communications (e.g., meeting scheduling, minor clarifications) do not need to be retained once their purpose has been fulfilled and relevant decisions or records are captured elsewhere.

---

#### **5\. Destruction of Records**

When records have met or exceeded the minimum retention period and are **not subject to a Litigation Hold or other legal requirement**, they may be destroyed as part of routine housekeeping.

* **Physical records** containing sensitive or confidential information (e.g., financial details, personal data) must be destroyed by shredding or other secure means.  
* **Electronic records** should be deleted in a manner that makes them reasonably inaccessible in ordinary course (e.g., deletion from shared drives and trash, removal from primary databases). Routine system backups that may still contain deleted records are acceptable so long as they are not used to circumvent this Policy.  
* Destruction should be **consistent and documented** at a high level (e.g., “Annual records cleanup – 2017 financial backup files removed from Drive on \[date\]”).

No records covered by a **Litigation Hold** may be destroyed until the hold is lifted by the Board or legal counsel.

---

#### **6\. Policy Review and Amendments**

The Treasurer and/or Secretary shall review this Policy at least **every three (3) years** or sooner if:

* The Organization’s size or complexity changes substantially;  
* Funders, auditors, or regulators require different retention periods; or  
* Legal counsel recommends changes.

Any proposed amendments shall be submitted to the Board for approval. Until amended or replaced, this Policy remains in full force and effect.
