Anti-Harassment and Anti-Discrimination Policy

Disagree Wisely, Inc.

Effective Date: August 10, 2025

Last Revised: August 10, 2025

1. Policy Statement

Disagree Wisely, Inc. is committed to maintaining a workplace free from harassment and discrimination. We provide equal employment opportunities to all employees, contractors, and volunteers regardless of protected characteristics.

2. Scope

This policy applies to:

  • All employees, officers, and directors
  • Contractors, consultants, and volunteers
  • All work-related settings (virtual or in-person)
  • Business-related activities and communications

3. Protected Characteristics

Disagree Wisely prohibits harassment and discrimination based on:

  • Race, color, ethnicity, or national origin
  • Religion or religious beliefs
  • Sex, gender, gender identity, or sexual orientation
  • Age
  • Physical or mental disability
  • Pregnancy or related medical conditions
  • Military or veteran status
  • Any other characteristic protected by law

4. Prohibited Conduct

Harassment includes:

  • Offensive comments, jokes, or slurs
  • Threats or intimidation
  • Unwanted physical contact
  • Display of offensive materials
  • Any conduct that creates a hostile work environment

Sexual Harassment includes:

  • Unwelcome sexual advances
  • Requests for sexual favors
  • Verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature
  • Making employment decisions based on submission to or rejection of such conduct

Discrimination includes:

  • Unfair treatment in hiring, firing, or promotions
  • Unequal compensation or work assignments
  • Denial of reasonable accommodations for disabilities

5. Reporting Violations

Employees, contractors, and volunteers who experience or witness harassment or discrimination should:

  • Contact their supervisor (if appropriate)
  • Contact the President: Simon Cullen (simon@disagreewisely.org)

6. No Retaliation

Disagree Wisely prohibits retaliation against anyone who:

  • Makes a good faith complaint
  • Participates in an investigation
  • Opposes discriminatory practices

7. Investigation and Response

Upon receiving a complaint, we will:

  • Acknowledge receipt promptly
  • Conduct a fair and timely investigation
  • Maintain confidentiality to the extent possible
  • Take appropriate corrective action if violations are found

Corrective actions may include:

  • Warning or counseling
  • Training requirements
  • Suspension or termination

8. Reasonable Accommodations

Employees with disabilities may request reasonable accommodations by contacting their supervisor or the President. We will engage in an interactive process to determine appropriate accommodations.

9. Compliance

This policy complies with applicable federal laws and the laws of states where we have employees, including:

  • Title VII of the Civil Rights Act
  • Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)
  • State civil rights laws
