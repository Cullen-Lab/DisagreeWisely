# Chief Technology & Operations Officer (CTOO)
## Disagree Wisely, Inc.


**Position Title:** Chief Technology & Operations Officer (CTOO)  
**Reports To:** President & CEO (Simon Cullen)  
**FLSA Status:** Full-time, Exempt
**Average Hours Worked:** 40 hrs/week


## Position Summary

As CTOO, you spearhead the design, development, and deployment of Disagree Wisely's Sway platform while running the operational engine that keeps Disagree Wisely humming.

You partner with the President on strategy, lead all software architecture and R&D, manage budgets, oversee compliance, and co-serve (with the President) as Disagree Wisely and Sway's public face in workshops, podcasts, and media appearances.

Fully remote; no travel required.

## Key Responsibilities

### Technology & Product Leadership
- Architect and evolve the Sway platform's technical stack
- Hands-on prototyping and feature development; oversee contract engineers and code reviews
- Maintain cybersecurity, data privacy, uptime, and scalability

### Operations & Finance Management
- Build and manage annual budgets, forecasts, cash-flow models
- Oversee accounting, payroll, vendor contracts, and insurance
- Ensure compliance with all federal and state nonprofit requirements, including 501(c)(3) reporting

### Research & Impact Evaluation
- Design and supervise studies on discussion quality and learning outcomes
- Analyze platform data; publish papers and technical memos
- Coordinate external research partnerships

### Instructor Collaboration & Support
- Work directly with educators to customize Sway for their courses
- Develop training materials, workshops, and user guides for instructors
- Collect feedback and iterate on features to improve teaching effectiveness

### Public Engagement & Outreach
- Represent Disagree Wisely in podcasts, webinars, and workshops
- Host or co-lead workshops for educators, analysts, and partner organizations
- Develop technical demos and training materials for users and funders

### Strategic & Governance Support
- Collaborate with the President on organizational strategy and grant proposals
- Prepare board materials, track KPIs, and brief directors on technical progress
- Advise on hiring for engineering, data-science, and operations roles

## Required Qualifications

- Advanced degrees in relevant analytical fields (e.g., Philosophy, Computer Science, Physics) or equivalent experience
- Deep hands-on coding and architecture experience
- Demonstrated success managing start-up or nonprofit operations (budgeting, payroll, compliance, vendor management)
- Experience designing and interpreting empirical research; strong data-analysis skills
- Exceptional written and verbal communication skills; comfortable in media and workshop settings

## Work Environment

Fully remote; no routine travel required

## Classification & Compensation

- Exempt salaried position; at-will employment
- Salary set by the Board using IRC §4958 comparability data (CTO, COO, Program Director) and reviewed annually
