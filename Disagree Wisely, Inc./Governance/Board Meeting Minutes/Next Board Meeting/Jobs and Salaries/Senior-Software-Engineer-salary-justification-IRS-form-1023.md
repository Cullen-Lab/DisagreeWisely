# Form 1023 Salary Justification

**Position**: Senior Software Engineer  
**Employee**: [To Be Determined]  
**Proposed Total Compensation**: $100,000 ($85,000 base + $15,000 benefits)  
**Compensation Start Date**: Year 2 (when projected revenue reaches $750,000)  
**Organization**: Disagree Wisely, Inc.

## Executive Summary

The Board of Directors has set compensation for the Senior Software Engineer position following IRC Section 4958 requirements. This senior technical leadership role involves managing a team of 3+ software engineers while scaling the existing Sway platform architecture to support hundreds of thousands and eventually millions of users. The Board reviewed comparable nonprofit and educational technology compensation data, obtained independent approval, and documented the process contemporaneously.

## Position Scope and Responsibilities

The Senior Software Engineer position requires 40 hours per week and encompasses:
- Leading technical development and scaling of the established Sway platform
- Managing and mentoring a team of 3+ software engineers
- Collaborating directly with CEO and CTOO on strategic technical decisions
- Scaling Flask microservices architecture to support hundreds of thousands of users
- Enhancing multi-LLM integration systems and real-time collaboration features
- Optimizing Google Cloud Platform infrastructure for educational environments

## Justification for $100,000 Compensation

- **Revenue Appropriate**: At Year 2 revenue of $750K, $100K represents 13.3% of revenue, within reasonable nonprofit standards
- **Below Comparable Organizations**: $100K is significantly below Unify America's technology roles ($151K-$157K range) despite similar mission focus
- **Educational Mission Alignment**: Compensation reflects nonprofit premium while attracting senior technical talent
- **Team Leadership Premium**: Appropriate compensation for managing 3+ engineers and strategic technical responsibilities
- **Deferred Start**: Organization demonstrates fiscal prudence by not hiring until Year 2 when revenue supports the position
- **Platform Scaling Requirements**: Technical complexity of scaling to millions of users justifies senior-level compensation
- **Conservative Compensation**: 35% below comparable nonprofit technology roles and significantly below for-profit senior software engineer averages ($147K-$189K nationally)

## Technical Complexity Justification

The Sway platform represents enterprise-grade educational technology requiring senior expertise:
- Flask 3.0+ microservices architecture with real-time collaboration systems
- Multi-LLM integration (OpenAI, Anthropic) with intelligent switching capabilities
- Google Cloud Platform deployment with Kubernetes auto-scaling
- WebSocket systems with Redis message queues supporting thousands of concurrent users
- Complex student matching algorithms and educational analytics systems
- FERPA-compliant data handling for educational environments

## Board Approval Process

The Board followed IRC 4958 rebuttable presumption procedures:
1. Full Board approved compensation with independent director Dr. Neil Thomason and CEO Dr. Simon Cullen voting (2 of 3 directors)
2. Obtained and reviewed current comparability data from similar educational and nonprofit technology organizations
3. Documented decision-making process in board minutes
4. Set compensation at market-competitive level while maintaining nonprofit mission alignment

## Conclusion

The $100,000 total compensation for the Senior Software Engineer position is reasonable and necessary, appropriately balancing the specialized technical requirements and team leadership responsibilities with organizational sustainability. This compensation reflects the unique demands of scaling sophisticated educational technology infrastructure while maintaining the nonprofit mission focus. The compensation package complies with all IRS requirements for tax-exempt organizations and demonstrates prudent fiscal management.

Supporting documentation and detailed comparability data are maintained in organizational records and available upon request.
