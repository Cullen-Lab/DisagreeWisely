# Form 1023 Salary Justification

**Position**: Chief Technology & Operations Officer (CTOO)  
**Employee**: Dr. Nicholas DiBella  
**Proposed Total Compensation**: $110,000 ($93,500 base + $16,500 benefits)  
**Compensation Timeline**: Partial Year 1 (few months), 11 months Year 2, Full Year 3+  
**Organization**: Disagree Wisely, Inc.

## Executive Summary

The Board of Directors has set compensation for the Chief Technology & Operations Officer position following IRC Section 4958 requirements. This dual executive role combines technology leadership and operational management for the Sway educational dialogue platform. The Board reviewed comparable nonprofit compensation data, obtained independent approval, and documented the process contemporaneously.

## Current Compensation Context

Dr. DiBella currently earns $92,000 total compensation (including benefits) as an IC Postdoctoral Fellow at Carnegie Mellon University. Beginning with partial-year employment in Year 1, the proposed $110,000 annual compensation represents a 19.6% increase reflecting the transition to significant executive responsibilities including technology development, operations management, financial oversight, and public representation.

## Justification for $100,000 Compensation

- **Scaled Appropriately**: At smaller revenue in early years, $110K represents prudent compensation that scales with organizational growth
- **Appropriate Increase**: 19.6% above current academic position reflecting executive transition and dual-role responsibilities
- **Revenue Appropriate**: Pro-rated compensation in Year 1, then represents 14.7% of Year 2 revenue ($750,000), decreasing to 11% by Year 3 ($1,000,000+)
- **Phased Implementation**: Starting with partial-year employment demonstrates fiscal prudence, with full compensation only as revenue grows
- **Mission-Driven**: Conservative compensation reflects commitment to organizational sustainability, maximizing resources for program development
- **Appropriate for Role**: Compensation reflects the CTOO's significant but focused responsibilities in technology and operations

## Board Approval Process

The Board followed IRC 4958 rebuttable presumption procedures:
1. Full Board approved compensation with independent director Dr. Neil Thomason and President Dr. Simon Cullen voting (2 of 3 directors)
2. Obtained and reviewed current comparability data from similar nonprofits
3. Documented decision-making process in board minutes
4. Set compensation at a conservative level appropriate for organizational size and mission

## Conclusion

The $110,000 total compensation for the CTOO position is reasonable and necessary, appropriately balancing the specialized nature of the role with organizational sustainability. This approach reflects the unique requirements of leading technology and operations for an educational dialogue platform. The compensation package complies with all IRS requirements for tax-exempt organizations and demonstrates prudent fiscal management.

Supporting documentation and detailed comparability data are maintained in organizational records and available upon request.
