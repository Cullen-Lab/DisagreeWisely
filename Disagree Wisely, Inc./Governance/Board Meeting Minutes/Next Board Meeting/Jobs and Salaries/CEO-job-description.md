# Chief Executive Officer (CEO)
## Disagree Wisely, Inc.

**Position Title:** Chief Executive Officer (CEO)  
**Reports To:** Board of Directors  
**FLSA Status:** Full-time, Exempt (≈ 60–80 hrs/week)

## Position Summary

As Chief Executive Officer (CEO) of Disagree Wisely, you provide overall vision, public leadership, and external representation for the organization and its flagship Sway platform. Working closely with the CTOO, you co-develop long-range strategy, spearhead fundraising and partnerships, guide research priorities, and serve as the primary public face of Disagree Wisely in media, conferences, and high-level stakeholder engagements. The role is fully remote; location may change without affecting duties.

## Key Responsibilities

### Strategic Leadership & Vision
- Lead strategic planning in partnership with the CTOO and Board
- Translate mission and research insights into organizational goals
- Monitor sector trends and position Disagree Wisely as a thought leader

### Fundraising & External Relations
- Create and execute multi-year fundraising strategy (foundations, major donors, sponsorships)
- Cultivate and steward donor relationships; draft high-impact grant proposals
- Represent Disagree Wisely in conferences, podcasts, media interviews, and panels

### Program & Research Oversight
- Co-direct research agenda with the CTOO; ensure findings inform platform enhancements
- Review and approve study designs, publications, and impact reports
- Champion evidence-based practices for constructive disagreement in education

### Partnership Development
- Forge alliances with universities, think tanks, nonprofits, and public agencies
- Negotiate MOUs and data-sharing agreements that accelerate adoption of Sway

### Governance & Board Relations
- Prepare agendas and materials; provide timely, data-driven updates to the Board
- Ensure effective board recruitment, orientation, and engagement
- Maintain adherence to bylaws, conflict-of-interest policy, and all IRS requirements

### Organizational Management
- Provide executive oversight of finance, HR, legal, and risk management (in coordination with the CTOO)
- Lead performance management and professional development for senior staff
- Foster an inclusive, mission-focused culture in a fully remote environment

## Required Qualifications

- Advanced degrees in relevant fields (e.g., Philosophy, Education, Public Policy, or related discipline), or equivalent experience
- Demonstrated success in nonprofit or start-up leadership, including fundraising and strategic partnerships
- Strong public-speaking and media skills; proven ability to communicate complex ideas to diverse audiences
- Experience overseeing research or program evaluation projects
- Familiarity with technology-enabled learning tools and basic product-development cycles
- Exceptional interpersonal, negotiation, and written communication abilities

## Work Environment

Fully remote; no routine travel required, although occasional conference appearances may occur subject to funding and health considerations.

## Classification & Compensation

- Exempt salaried position; at-will employment
- Salary to be set by the Board using IRC §4958 comparability data (CEO/Executive Director benchmarks) and reviewed annually
