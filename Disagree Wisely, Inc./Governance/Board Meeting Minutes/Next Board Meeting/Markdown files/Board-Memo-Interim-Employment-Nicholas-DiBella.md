# MEMORANDUM TO BOARD OF DIRECTORS

**TO:** Board of Directors, Disagree Wisely, Inc.  
**FROM:** Simon Cullen, President  
**DATE:** [Meeting Date]  
**RE:** Interim Employment Arrangement for Dr. Nicholas DiBella

## Executive Summary

This memo requests Board approval for an interim employment arrangement with Dr. Nicholas DiBella due to the indefinite suspension of his federally funded postdoctoral position. The proposed arrangement maintains operational continuity while ensuring IRS compliance and organizational stability.

## Background

Dr. DiBella currently serves as Secretary, Treasurer, and co-founder of Disagree Wisely, Inc. His Intelligence Community Postdoctoral Research Fellowship at Carnegie Mellon University, which provided his primary income, has been indefinitely paused due to federal funding issues beyond his control.

**Original Timeline:**
- Postdoc position scheduled to end: September 30, 2025
- CTOO employment with Disagree Wisely: Originally planned for 2026

**Current Situation:**
- Postdoc salary suspended: Effective immediately
- Dr. DiBella continues essential work for Disagree Wisely without compensation
- Uncertainty regarding postdoc resumption

## Proposed Interim Arrangement

**Structure:** Temporary employment agreement  
**Start Date:** May 28, 2025  
**End Date:** Earlier of (1) postdoc position resumption or (2) September 30, 2025  
**Compensation:** $92,000 annualized (equivalent to current postdoc), pro-rated  
**Role:** Interim Technology & Operations Officer

## Justification

### Operational Necessity
- Dr. DiBella performs critical functions including platform development, grant management, and partnership coordination
- His specialized knowledge of Sway's architecture and research protocols is irreplaceable in the short term
- Continued unpaid service creates personal financial hardship and organizational risk

### Compensation Reasonableness
- $92,000 matches his current academic qualification level
- Identical to his previous compensation, demonstrating no excess benefit
- Well within market range for his experience and responsibilities
- Pro-rated structure ensures cost control

### IRS Compliance
- Separate from previously approved CTOO compensation plan
- Clear documentation of extraordinary circumstances
- Temporary nature with defined end points
- Board approval follows IRC 4958 procedures

## Financial Impact

**Maximum Cost:** $31,759 (May 28 - Sept 30, 2025 = 4.14 months)  
**Monthly Cost:** $7,667  
**Funding Source:** Operating reserves and current grants

This expenditure is within our approved budget parameters and does not affect our ability to meet program commitments or maintain required reserves.

## Risk Analysis

**Risk of Not Approving:**
- Loss of critical institutional knowledge
- Potential disruption to platform development and partnerships
- Personal financial hardship for key founder
- Difficulty maintaining grant compliance and reporting

**Risk of Approving:**
- Minimal financial risk given short duration and reasonable compensation
- Well-documented extraordinary circumstances justify deviation from original plan
- Clear termination provisions limit organizational exposure

## Legal and Governance Considerations

1. **Conflict of Interest:** Dr. DiBella will recuse himself from voting on this matter
2. **Independent Approval:** Requires majority vote of disinterested directors
3. **Documentation:** Comprehensive agreement with clear terms and conditions
4. **Precedent:** Clearly documented as extraordinary circumstances, not standard practice

## Recommendation

I recommend the Board approve this interim employment arrangement. The proposal:
- Addresses legitimate organizational needs
- Provides reasonable compensation under extraordinary circumstances
- Maintains IRS compliance through proper procedures
- Includes appropriate safeguards and termination provisions
