# BOARD RESOLUTION
## Disagree Wisely, Inc.

**Resolution Number:** [2025-06]  
**Date:** [Meeting Date]  
**Subject:** Interim Benefits Package for Simon Cullen

---

**WHEREAS**, Disagree Wisely, Inc. ("Organization") is a Florida nonprofit corporation seeking recognition of tax-exempt status under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code;

**WHEREAS**, Dr. Simon Cullen serves as President and has been formally appointed as Chief Executive Officer of the Organization;

**WHEREAS**, Dr. Cullen has been providing executive leadership services to the Organization without compensation while the Organization establishes its operations and revenue streams;

**WHEREAS**, Dr. Cullen requires interim benefits support during the period before the Organization can provide comprehensive executive compensation;

**WHEREAS**, the Board finds it appropriate to provide interim benefits assistance to retain executive leadership during this critical organizational development phase;

**WHEREAS**, the Board has reviewed comparable data regarding executive benefits provided by similar nonprofit organizations through Form 990 analysis;

**WHEREAS**, the Board seeks to ensure compliance with all applicable laws, including Internal Revenue Code Sections 4958 and 4941 regarding reasonable compensation and self-dealing;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED** that the Board of Directors hereby approves the following:

## Section 1: Interim Benefits Package Authorization
The Board hereby authorizes a taxable benefits package reimbursement for Dr. Simon Cullen in the amount of One Thousand Dollars ($1,000) per month to assist with health insurance, retirement contributions, and life insurance costs.

## Section 2: Effective Period
This health insurance reimbursement shall be effective for the period from June 1, 2025 through August 31, 2025, for a total reimbursement of Three Thousand Dollars ($3,000).

## Section 3: Tax Treatment
This benefit shall be treated as taxable compensation subject to:
- Federal income tax withholding
- Federal Insurance Contributions Act (FICA) taxes
- Pennsylvania state income tax withholding
- All applicable payroll tax reporting requirements

## Section 4: Payment Processing
Reimbursement payments shall be processed through the Organization's Gusto payroll system with appropriate tax withholdings and shall appear on Dr. Cullen's Form W-2 as taxable income.

## Section 5: Reasonableness Determination
The Board hereby finds this reimbursement to be reasonable and necessary based on:
- Comparable executive benefits provided by similar nonprofit organizations (significantly below market rates of $2,069-$2,979/month)
- The interim nature of this arrangement pending establishment of comprehensive executive compensation
- Dr. Cullen's ongoing executive responsibilities without salary compensation

## Section 6: Automatic Termination
This reimbursement shall automatically terminate if:
- Dr. Cullen obtains benefits coverage (health insurance, retirement, life insurance) through another employer
- Dr. Cullen receives other compensation that includes comprehensive benefits
- The Organization implements a comprehensive benefits program

## Section 7: Employment Documentation
Dr. Cullen shall complete all required employment documentation including:
- Form W-4 (Employee's Withholding Certificate)
- Form I-9 (Employment Eligibility Verification)  
- Pennsylvania new hire reporting requirements

## Section 8: IRC Compliance
The Board hereby makes the following determinations required under Internal Revenue Code Sections 4958 and 4941:

a) **Independent Judgment:** This decision was made by disinterested directors after full consideration of the relevant facts and circumstances.

b) **Comparability Data:** The Board reviewed and considered comprehensive benefits data (health insurance, retirement, life insurance) for similar executive positions at comparable organizations.

c) **Contemporaneous Documentation:** This resolution and supporting materials constitute contemporaneous documentation of the decision-making process.

d) **Reasonableness:** The Board finds the benefit to be reasonable in light of Dr. Cullen's responsibilities and the interim nature of the arrangement.

## Section 9: Conflict of Interest
Dr. Simon Cullen recused himself from all discussions and voting regarding this matter due to his direct financial interest. The resolution was approved by disinterested directors only.

## Section 10: Internal Controls
All reimbursement payments shall require approval by disinterested directors in accordance with the Organization's conflict of interest policies, ensuring Dr. Cullen does not approve his own reimbursement payments.
