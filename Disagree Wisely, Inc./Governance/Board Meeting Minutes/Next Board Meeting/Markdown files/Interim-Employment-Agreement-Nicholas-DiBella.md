# INTERIM EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT

**Disagree Wisely, Inc.**  
**Interim Technology & Operations Officer Position**

---

**PARTIES:**
- **Employer:** Disagree Wisely, Inc., a Florida nonprofit corporation ("Organization")
- **Employee:** Dr. Nicholas DiBella ("Employee")

**EFFECTIVE DATE:** May 28, 2025

---

## ARTICLE 1: EMPLOYMENT AND TERM

### 1.1 Position
Employee is hereby employed as Interim Technology & Operations Officer of the Organization.

### 1.2 Term
This Agreement shall commence on May 28, 2025, and shall terminate on the earliest of:
- The date Employee's Intelligence Community Postdoctoral Research Fellowship resumes;
- September 30, 2025; or
- Earlier termination in accordance with this Agreement.

### 1.3 Extraordinary Circumstances
The parties acknowledge this Agreement is entered into due to the extraordinary circumstance of the indefinite suspension of Employee's federally funded postdoctoral position.

## ARTICLE 2: DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

### 2.1 Primary Responsibilities
Employee shall serve as Interim Technology & Operations Officer with duties including:
- Platform development, maintenance, and technical architecture oversight
- Operations management and organizational coordination
- Grant administration and compliance activities
- Partnership development and stakeholder relations
- Financial oversight and administrative functions
- Such other duties as assigned by the Chief Executive Officer consistent with the position

### 2.2 Performance Standards
Employee shall perform duties with the skill, care, and diligence expected of a professional in a similar position and shall comply with all Organization policies and procedures.

### 2.3 Time Commitment
This is a full-time position requiring approximately 40 hours per week, with flexibility for the demands of nonprofit operations.

## ARTICLE 3: COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS

### 3.1 Base Salary
Employee shall receive an annual salary of Ninety-Two Thousand Dollars ($92,000), pro-rated for the actual period of employment and payable through the Organization's Gusto payroll system. Payments will be processed by the Chief Executive Officer following completion of required tax registrations (myPATH and EFTPS).

### 3.2 Benefits
Employee shall be eligible for benefits as determined by the Organization's standard policies for employees, which may include health insurance and other benefits as available.

### 3.3 No Double Compensation
Employee acknowledges that acceptance of compensation under this Agreement precludes simultaneous compensation from any other source for the same work period.

## ARTICLE 4: TERMINATION

### 4.1 Automatic Termination
This Agreement shall automatically terminate without notice upon:
- Resumption of Employee's postdoctoral fellowship
- September 30, 2025
- Employee's acceptance of other full-time employment

### 4.2 Termination by Organization
Organization may terminate this Agreement at any time with thirty (30) days written notice.

### 4.3 Termination by Employee
Employee may terminate this Agreement at any time with fourteen (14) days written notice.

### 4.4 Compensation Upon Termination
Upon termination, Employee shall be entitled to compensation earned through the last day of employment.

## ARTICLE 5: TRANSITION AND CONTINUITY

### 5.1 Transition to CTOO Role
The parties acknowledge that Employee may transition to the Chief Technology & Operations Officer role in the future under separate employment terms, pending Board approval and organizational circumstances.

### 5.2 Knowledge Transfer
Upon termination of this Agreement, Employee agrees to provide reasonable cooperation in transferring responsibilities and knowledge to ensure organizational continuity.

## ARTICLE 6: CONFIDENTIALITY AND INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 6.1 Confidential Information
Employee acknowledges access to confidential information and agrees to maintain its confidentiality during and after employment.

### 6.2 Work Product
All work product, inventions, and intellectual property created during employment shall be the property of the Organization.

## ARTICLE 7: GENERAL PROVISIONS

### 7.1 Entire Agreement
This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, or agreements relating to the subject matter herein.

### 7.2 Amendment
This Agreement may be amended only by written agreement signed by both parties.

### 7.3 Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Florida.

### 7.4 Severability
If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

### 7.5 Board Approval
This Agreement is subject to and contingent upon approval by the Board of Directors of the Organization.
