# CEO Health Insurance Justification
## Health Insurance Reimbursement for Simon Cullen

**Position**: Chief Executive Officer (CEO)  
**Employee**: Dr. Simon Cullen  
**Proposed Health Insurance Reimbursement**: $1,000 per month ($12,000 annually)  
**Reimbursement Period**: June 1, 2025 - August 31, 2025 (3 months = $3,000 total)  
**Organization**: Disagree Wisely, Inc.

## Executive Summary

The Board of Directors has approved health insurance reimbursement for the Chief Executive Officer position following IRC Section 4958 requirements. Dr. Cullen serves as CEO without salary compensation while providing extensive executive leadership during the organization's critical development phase. This health insurance reimbursement provides essential support while maintaining fiscal responsibility and compliance with nonprofit compensation standards.

## Position Scope and Responsibilities

The CEO position requires 60-80 hours per week and encompasses:
- Strategic planning and organizational vision
- Leading all fundraising efforts (foundations, major donors, partnerships)
- Primary media and public representation
- Research oversight and publication
- Board relations and governance
- Partnership development with universities and educational organizations
- Executive oversight of technology development and operations

## Comparable Organization Analysis

The Board reviewed CEO health insurance and benefits data from organizations with similar missions focused on dialogue, depolarization, and educational innovation:

| Organization | 2023 Revenue | Position | Annual Benefits | Monthly Benefits |
|--------------|-------------|----------|----------------|------------------|
| **Constructive Dialogue Institute** | $1.3M | Executive Director | $24,826 | $2,069 |
| **Heterodox Academy** | $2.98M | Chief Executive Officer | $35,750 | $2,979 |
| **Braver Angels** | $5.03M | President/Executive Director | $31,479 | $2,623 |

**Source Documentation**: Form 990 filings for 2022-2023 reporting periods.

**Revenue Context**: Disagree Wisely's current revenue level makes these organizations appropriate comparables for benefits benchmarking, particularly Constructive Dialogue Institute with similar organizational size.

**Reimbursement Scope**: The $1,000/month covers health insurance, retirement contributions, and life insurance - mirroring a standard academic benefits package structure.

## Justification for $1,000 Monthly Reimbursement

- **Significantly Below Comparable Organizations**: $1,000/month is 52-67% below comparable CEO benefits at similar mission organizations ($2,069-$2,979/month range)
- **No Salary Compensation**: Benefits provide essential support while CEO forgoes salary compensation during critical startup phase
- **Revenue Appropriate**: $12,000 annually represents conservative benefits level for organization's current revenue
- **Interim Nature**: Three-month period demonstrates fiscal prudence and temporary assistance approach
- **Workload Justified**: 60-80 hour weeks represent 1.5-2x standard full-time commitment without salary compensation
- **Mission-Driven Approach**: Conservative benefits reflect commitment to organizational sustainability while retaining executive leadership

## Reimbursement Structure

### Reimbursement Components
The $1,000 monthly package provides support for:
- Health insurance premiums and medical costs
- Retirement plan contributions (401k/403b equivalent)
- Life insurance premiums

### Tax Treatment
- Reimbursement treated as taxable compensation subject to:
  - Federal income tax withholding
  - Federal Insurance Contributions Act (FICA) taxes
  - Pennsylvania state income tax withholding
  - All applicable payroll tax reporting requirements

### Administrative Implementation
- Processed through Gusto payroll system with appropriate tax withholdings
- Monthly payment schedule with proper documentation
- Appears on CEO's Form W-2 as taxable reimbursement

### Internal Controls
- Disinterested director approval required for each monthly payment
- CEO cannot approve own benefit payments per conflict of interest policy
- Proper documentation and audit trail maintained

## Board Approval Process

The Board followed IRC 4958 rebuttable presumption procedures:
1. Full Board approved benefits with independent director Dr. Neil Thomason and Secretary/Treasurer Dr. Nicholas DiBella voting (2 of 3 directors)
2. Obtained and reviewed current comparability data from similar nonprofits via Form 990 filings
3. Documented decision-making process in board minutes
4. Set benefits significantly below market to ensure reasonableness

## Automatic Termination Provisions

This health insurance reimbursement shall automatically terminate if:
- CEO obtains comprehensive benefits coverage (health insurance, retirement, life insurance) through another employer
- CEO receives other compensation that includes comprehensive health insurance coverage
- Organization implements comprehensive executive compensation program
- Three-month interim period concludes (August 31, 2025)

## IRC 4958 Compliance

### Independent Judgment
This decision was made by disinterested directors after full consideration of relevant facts and circumstances, with CEO recusal from all discussions and voting.

### Comparability Data
The Board relied on specific Form 990 data from comparable organizations showing comprehensive benefits packages (health insurance, retirement, life insurance):
- **Constructive Dialogue Institute**: $24,826 annual benefits ($2,069/month)
- **Heterodox Academy**: $35,750 annual benefits ($2,979/month)  
- **Braver Angels**: $31,479 annual benefits ($2,623/month)

### Contemporaneous Documentation
This resolution and supporting materials constitute contemporaneous documentation of the decision-making process with detailed comparability analysis.

### Reasonableness Determination
The Board finds the $1,000 monthly benefits to be reasonable based on:
- Comparable CEO benefits at similar organizations (52-67% below market rates)
- Interim nature pending comprehensive compensation structure
- CEO's extensive responsibilities without salary compensation
- Conservative approach demonstrating fiscal responsibility

## Conclusion

The $1,000 monthly health insurance reimbursement for the CEO position is reasonable and necessary, providing essential interim support while maintaining fiscal responsibility. This approach is significantly below comparable organizations while recognizing the CEO's extensive contributions without salary compensation. The reimbursement complies with all IRS requirements for tax-exempt organizations and demonstrates prudent fiscal management during the organization's development phase.

Supporting documentation and detailed comparability data are maintained in organizational records and available upon request.
