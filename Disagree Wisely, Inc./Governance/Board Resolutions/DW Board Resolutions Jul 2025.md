---

### **1\. Appointment of Chief Technology & Operations Officer (Resolution 2025-03)**

**RESOLVED**, that Dr. Nicholas DiBella is hereby appointed Chief Technology & Operations Officer (CTOO) of the Corporation, effective July 11 2025, to serve until his successor is elected or until earlier resignation or removal by the Board; and

**RESOLVED FURTHER**, that the CTOO shall have the duties and authority outlined in the job description reviewed by the Board and shall report to the President.

---

### **2\. Interim Compensation (Resolution 2025-04)**

**WHEREAS** Dr. DiBella’s federally funded post-doctoral fellowship was suspended effective May 28 2025, creating an unforeseen loss of income; and  
**WHEREAS** the Board has reviewed the attached “Interim Compensation Resolution & Justification,” including comparability data from peer organizations and confirmation that the proposed amount is well below market rates;

**RESOLVED**, that, beginning May 28 2025 and ending the earlier of (a) September 30 2025 or (b) the resumption of Dr. DiBella’s fellowship, the Corporation shall pay Dr. DiBella interim wages **pro-rated at an annualized rate of $92,000**, with back pay for work performed since May 28 2025; and

**RESOLVED FURTHER**, that this temporary arrangement is approved as reasonable compensation under IRC § 4958, having been approved by a majority of disinterested directors after review of appropriate comparability data and documentation; and

**RESOLVED FURTHER**, that the officers of the Corporation are authorized and directed to execute all documents and take all actions necessary to implement these resolutions.

---

### **3\. General**

**RESOLVED**, that this written consent may be executed in counterparts (including by PDF or electronic signature) and, when executed by all directors, shall be filed with the minutes and treated as action taken at a duly convened meeting of the Board.

## **Executive Summary**

The Board seeks to approve interim compensation for Dr. Nicholas DiBella during the period of due to extraordinary circumstances: the indefinite suspension of his federally funded postdoctoral fellowship. The proposed $92,000 annual rate (pro-rated) maintains his current compensation level while ensuring critical organizational continuity.

## **Extraordinary Circumstances**

### **Background**

- Dr. DiBella’s Post-Doctoral Fellowship was scheduled to conclude September 30, 2025\. This Fellowship was Dr DiBella’s only source of income.  
- He was informed by ORISE of a “Stop Work Order starting May 28, 2025.”  
- Since his ORISE postdoc was made inactive, Dr DiBella has continued his full-time work with Disagree Wisely. He has not been paid for this work yet. 

### **Operational Necessity**

Dr. DiBella's continued involvement is critical for: 

- Sway platform development and technical maintenance  
- Grant compliance and administration  
- Partnership coordination with Heterodox Academy and other organizations  
- Financial oversight and operational management  
- Preparation for scaling operations

## **Compensation Analysis**

### **Proposed Structure**

- **Annual Rate:** $92,000 (identical to current postdoc compensation)  
- **Actual Period:** Approximately 4.14 months (May 28 \- September 30, 2025).  Back payment will be administered for the period going back to May 28, 2025\.  
- **Maximum Cost:** $31,759  
- **Pro-rated Structure:** Ensures cost control and fiscal responsibility

### **Reasonableness Determination**

#### **1\. Current Compensation Equivalence**

- $92,000 matches Dr. DiBella's existing IC Fellowship compensation  
- Thus, there is no increase above current qualified compensation level

#### **2\. Academic Comparability**

- IC Postdoctoral Fellowships represent competitive academic compensation  
- Fellowship was awarded through rigorous federal competition  
- Compensation reflects doctoral-level expertise in relevant fields

#### **3\. Comparable Organization Analysis**

The Board reviewed compensation data from organizations with similar missions focused on dialogue, depolarization, and educational technology:

| Organization | 2023 Revenue | Position | Total Compensation |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Heterodox Academy** | $2.98M | Director of Programs | $122,340 |
|  |  | (manages technology and programs) |  |
| **Braver Angels** | $5.03M | Chief Technology Officer | $151,038 |
|  |  | (handles tech and operations) |  |
| **Unify America** | $1.66M | Chief Operating Officer | $161,192 |
|  |  | (oversees operations and growth) |  |

**Analysis:** The proposed $92,000 compensation is significantly below comparable technology and operations leadership roles at similar mission-driven organizations (25-43% lower), demonstrating conservative and reasonable compensation.

#### **4\. Academic Credentials Context**

- Dual expertise (Physics B.S. from MIT, Philosophy Ph.D from Stanford)  
- Specialized knowledge in AI, educational technology, and dialogue systems  
- IC Fellowship demonstrates competitive federal assessment of qualifications at the post-doc level. 

#### **5\. Organizational Context**

- Essential for maintaining $500,000+ in active grants and commitments  
- Protects significant organizational investments in platform development  
- Preserves institutional knowledge critical for ongoing operations

## **Risk Mitigation**

### **Financial Safeguards**

- **Limited Duration:** Maximum 4.1 months exposure  
- **Clear Termination:** Automatic end upon fellowship resumption  
- **Pro-rated Structure:** Costs scale with actual employment period  
- **Budget Availability:** Well within current organizational reserves

### **Legal Compliance**

- **Extraordinary Circumstances:** Well-documented external cause beyond the organization’s control  
- **No Precedent:** Explicitly temporary arrangement due to unique circumstances  
- **Board Oversight:** Independent director review and approval  
- **Conflict Management:** Employee recusal from compensation decision

## **IRC 4958 Compliance**

### **Independent Review**

- Employee recused from all discussions and voting  
- Reviewed and approved by independent director Dr. Neil Thomason  
- Reviewed and approved by President Dr. Simon Cullen

### **Comparability Data**

- **Specific Comparable Organizations:** Heterodox Academy ($122,340), Braver Angels ($151,038), Unify America ($161,192) for similar technology/operations leadership roles  
- **Academic Fellowship:** IC Fellowship program represents competitive federal compensation determination at $92,000 annually  
- **Conservative Positioning:** Proposed compensation is 25-43% below comparable nonprofit technology leadership positions  
- **Revenue-Appropriate:** Compensation well within reasonable range for organization's current revenue level

### **Contemporaneous Documentation**

- Board resolution documenting extraordinary circumstances  
- Written employment agreement with clear terms  
- Comprehensive justification analysis (this document)

## **Conclusion**

The proposed interim compensation arrangement:

- Addresses legitimate organizational needs  
- Maintains compensation at current qualified level without increase  
- Is significantly below market rates for comparable technology leadership roles (25-43% lower than peer organizations)   
- Includes appropriate safeguards and termination provisions  
- Complies with all IRS requirements for nonprofit compensation decisions with robust comparability data  
- Demonstrates fiscal responsibility through limited duration and pro-rated structure

**Recommendation:** Approve interim compensation arrangement as outlined, with clear documentation of extraordinary circumstances and temporary nature.
