# Appendix A: Job Description

## Director of Communications

### Disagree Wisely, Inc.

---

Disagree Wisely seeks a Director of Communications to lead strategic communications, partnership development, and growth operations for Sway, our AI-powered platform that facilitates constructive dialogue between students with opposing viewpoints. This role is critical to achieving our ambitious scaling goals: expanding from 20,000 monthly active Sway users to 100,000+ within two years, supporting implementations across 125+ university campuses through major federal grants, and positioning Sway as the leading civil discourse education technology for higher education. Reporting to the Chief Technology and Operations Officer (CTOO), the Director will own external communications strategy—including instructor outreach, university and corporate partnership development, and grant-related external relations—while also managing internal operations essential to a small, but rapidly growing team. This includes overseeing support systems, coordinating with technical personnel on product development (including our forthcoming ChatMaps real-time argument visualization feature), managing social media and content strategy, and supporting hiring efforts as we scale. The ideal candidate brings deep experience translating complex AI and research concepts for diverse audiences, a track record of driving adoption of emerging technologies in mission-driven contexts, and the versatility to manage multiple concurrent workstreams while keeping strategic growth objectives in focus.

The Director of Communications must combine the strategic rigor of nonprofit communications leadership with the operational agility of an early-stage organization. The ideal candidate brings fluency in academic culture and demonstrated success engaging faculty and institutional leadership. They must have experience synthesizing research and impact data into narratives tailored for funders, university stakeholders, and potential platform partners. The ideal candidate has deep AI literacy and a track record of building buy-in for emerging technologies among hesitant audiences—experience that translates directly to addressing instructor concerns and engaging confidently with both technical and non-technical collaborators. Strong project management capabilities and experience coordinating distributed teams across multiple concurrent workstreams are essential. This position offers the opportunity to shape communications and growth strategy at a pivotal moment for an organization with significant grant funding, growing national visibility, and the potential to transform how millions of students learn to engage across difference.
