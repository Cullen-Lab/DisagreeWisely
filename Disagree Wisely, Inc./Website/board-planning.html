3. 501(c)(3) & Restructuring Update Simon — 15 min

This is the most substantive agenda item. Full transparency with the board on the compliance situation.

The Restructuring

  • DW was originally intended to house both research/education and Sway development
  • Foundation Group (our 501(c)(3) advisors) recommended a clean separation: DW focuses on research, education, and program delivery; Sway IP and development move to a separate for-profit PBC
  • This is a common nonprofit/for-profit structure and keeps DW’s tax-exempt status clean
  • DW accesses Sway for its programs under a free license from the co-founders (to be formalized with the PBC)

501(c)(3) Application Status

  • Foundation Group (liaison: Ren Henderson) is finalizing the IRS Form 1023 application
  • Target submission: mid-April 2026 (hopefully sooner)
  • Requesting expedited review from the IRS because the FIPSE grant (~$800K) cannot be disbursed until 501(c)(3) status is approved
  • A supporting letter from the FIPSE grantor (Notre Dame) has been requested

The Compliance Transition

Key issue for the board: The operational separation between DW and Sway development is not yet complete. During the transition, some development costs continue to flow through DW via the HxA/AVDF services agreement. The board should understand why and what is being done about it.
  • HxA/AVDF services agreement: The current Phase 2 agreement (Jan–Nov 2026) commits DW to “execute technical development” of Sway. This language predates the restructuring.
  • Amending the agreement: Simon proposed removing development language from DW’s scope. HxA Executive Director Michael Regnier responded (March 5) that this is not feasible with ~3 months remaining in the grant — it would require legal counsel review, HxA board COI approval (for Simon, Nick, and Mike Strambler), and AVDF approval for a material spending change.
  • Practical outcome: DW will continue to invoice HxA for some development-related costs likely through May or June 2026 (when the remaining AVDF development budget is expected to be used up). DW is fully reimbursed for these costs — the net financial impact on DW is zero.
  • Mitigating factors: The restructuring is genuine and proactive. DW voluntarily stopped spending the Learning Agency development funds (see below) before being told to do so. The 501(c)(3) application narrative frames DW broadly as a research and education organization.
  • For-profit PBC: Planned but not yet incorporated. Will hold Sway IP, handle product development, and license the platform to DW for research/education use at no cost. Simon and Nick will establish this in the coming months.

Learning Agency / Tools Competition Funds (~$125K)

  • Simon and Nick won the Learning Agency Tools Competition Growth Prize ($150K) for Sway development
  • $25K spent on Instructor Insights (complete). ~$125K remains earmarked for ChatMaps (not yet started)
  • Because DW is restructuring away from development, these funds can no longer be compliantly spent through DW
  • Plan: return the ~$125K to the Learning Agency for redirection to the for-profit PBC (or to Simon/Nick personally)
  • Simon and Nick met with Kristyn Manoukian (Learning Agency) on March 2. She was receptive and sees no major concerns. The funds were administered through Digital Harbor Foundation, so their finance team needs to weigh in on logistics.
  • Kristyn also indicated flexibility in how remaining Tools funds are used going forward (not restricted to ChatMaps development alone)
  • Financial impact on DW: When the ~$125K is returned, DW’s cash drops from ~$173K to ~$48K. This is the “effective working cash” figure already reflected in our financial overview.

4. Sway IP & CMU Dispute Simon — 5 min

This is Simon and Nick’s personal matter (not a DW matter, since DW does not own the Sway IP), but the board should be aware because DW’s programmatic work depends on access to Sway.

  • Background: Carnegie Mellon University has asserted a joint ownership interest in the Sway platform IP, based on claimed contributions by CMU staff and interns during the platform’s early development (2023–2024)
  • Simon and Nick’s position: They are the sole creators. Under CMU’s own IP policy (Section 3-6-1), educational courseware belongs to the creator “in all cases,” regardless of university sponsorship or use of facilities. CMU code contributions amount to 0.77% of the codebase (454 lines of commodity CSS, HTML, and boilerplate)
  • Legal counsel: David Kalson (Dentons) represented Simon and Nick through early 2026. He assessed the legal position as strong under the courseware exemption. After CMU’s General Counsel declined to waive ownership, Simon and Nick ended the Dentons engagement in February 2026 to pursue a different approach.
  • Current status: We are evaluating our options. CMU has been notified to expect direct contact or new representation. No formal legal proceedings have been initiated by either side.
  • Risk to DW: Low in the near term. Sway continues to operate and grow (80+ institutions, ~10,000 students). CMU has not taken any action to restrict Sway’s use. If the dispute were to escalate, it could create uncertainty around the IP that underpins DW’s programs, but this is considered unlikely given the strength of the courseware exemption argument.
  • Board input welcome: While this is technically not within DW’s purview (the IP is personally held), Simon and Nick welcome any ideas or counsel the board members may have.
