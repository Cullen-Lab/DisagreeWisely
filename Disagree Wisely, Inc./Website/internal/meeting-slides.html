Why We Are Restructuring

  • DW was originally intended to house research, education, and Sway development

  • Supporting letter from grantor (Notre Dame) requested

For-Profit PBC

  • Planned but not yet incorporated
  • Will hold Sway IP, handle product development
  • Will license the platform to DW for research/education at no cost

Sway IP & CMU Dispute
Simon — 5 min
6
This is Simon and Nick's personal matter (DW does not own Sway IP), but the board should be aware because DW's programs depend on Sway.

Background

  • CMU (via CTTEC and the General Counsel's office) asserts a joint ownership interest in Sway based on claimed contributions by CMU staff/interns during early development (2023–2024)
  • Simon and Nick assert sole ownership under CMU's IP Policy §3-6-1: educational courseware belongs to the creator "in all cases," regardless of university sponsorship or facilities
  • CMU-affiliated code in the current codebase: 0.77% (454 lines of commodity CSS, HTML, boilerplate)

Current Status

  • Legal counsel (David Kalson, Dentons) represented Simon and Nick through early 2026; assessed legal position as strong
  • After CMU's General Counsel declined to waive ownership, Simon and Nick ended the Dentons engagement in February 2026 to pursue a different approach
  • CMU has been notified to expect direct contact or new representation
  • We are evaluating our options. Board input is welcome, though this is technically not within DW's purview.

Risk to DW

  • Low in the near term. Sway continues to operate and grow. CMU has not taken any action to restrict use.
  • If the dispute escalated, it could create uncertainty around the IP that underpins DW's programs — but considered unlikely given the strength of the courseware exemption
