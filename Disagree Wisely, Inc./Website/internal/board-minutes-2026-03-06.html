3. 501(c)(3) & Restructuring Update

Restructuring: The President explained the structural separation between Disagree Wisely (nonprofit: research, education, program delivery) and Sway IP (to be held by a separate for-profit Public Benefit Corporation). This separation was recommended by Foundation Group to avoid private benefit concerns with the IRS. The PBC has not yet been incorporated but can be done for approximately $50 in Delaware.

501(c)(3) Application: Foundation Group has everything they need and is now producing the official application for submission in the coming weeks. Correction from prepared slides: the President stated that expedited IRS review will not be requested (revising the earlier plan). The application will go through standard review.

HxA/AVDF Compliance Transition: The current Phase 2 services agreement (Jan–Nov 2026) includes development language that predates the restructuring. The Secretary reported that DW had proposed an amendment to remove development costs from DW's scope, but HxA Executive Director Michael Regnier informed them the previous day that this is not feasible with approximately three months remaining in the grant — it would require legal counsel review, HxA board COI approval, and AVDF approval. The President elaborated that the practical outcome is that DW will continue invoicing HxA for some development-related costs through May/June 2026, when the remaining AVDF development budget is expected to be used up. The net financial cost to DW is zero (fully reimbursed).

4. Learning Agency / Tools Competition Funds (~$125K)

Simon and Nick won the Learning Agency Tools Competition Growth Prize ($150K) for Sway. $25K was spent on Instructor Insights as proposed. ~$125K remains earmarked for ChatMaps (a real-time argument visualization feature). Because DW is restructuring away from development, these funds cannot be compliantly spent on ChatMaps through DW.

The President reported that the original hope of returning the ~$125K to the Learning Agency for redirection to the PBC is not possible — the funds come from a bucket restricted to nonprofits. However, the Learning Agency is open to restructuring the award so that the funds can be used for other compliant purposes within DW (operational and scaling costs, research, infrastructure). The Learning Agency responded with "no concerns on our end regarding reallocation and the adjustments to your plan." The President will meet with them again to finalize the restructured agreement and report back to the board. Financial impact: the ~$125K is currently included in DW's gross cash (~$173K).

5. Sway IP & CMU Dispute

The President briefed the board on the CMU IP dispute. Carnegie Mellon has asserted a joint ownership interest in the Sway platform IP. The co-founders' position is that Sway qualifies as educational courseware under CMU's own IP policy (Section 3-6-1), which vests ownership in the creator "in all cases." CMU-affiliated code contributions amount to 0.77% of the codebase (454 lines of commodity CSS, HTML, and boilerplate). Previous counsel (David Kalson, Dentons) assessed the legal position as strong. The Dentons engagement ended in February 2026.

Board input:

  • Ken Stern recommended obtaining independent legal counsel (not Foundation Group) and offered to connect Simon with Laura Ellsworth at Jones Day as potential counsel.
  • Ken noted both IP law and employment law dimensions to the dispute.
  • The board discussed the option of removing the 0.77% CMU-affiliated code entirely.
