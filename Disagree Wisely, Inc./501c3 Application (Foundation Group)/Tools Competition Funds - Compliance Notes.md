# Tools Competition Funds — Compliance Notes

## Summary

Disagree Wisely (DW) received $150,000 in prize money from the Tools Competition for developing Sway. $25,000 has been spent on Sway IP development. The remaining $125,000 is unspent. This document outlines the compliance considerations and path forward.

---

## Background

### The Award

- **Amount:** $150,000
- **Source:** Tools Competition (global competition)
- **Winners:** Nick DiBella and Simon Cullen
- **Official Awardee:** Disagree Wisely, Inc.
- **Purpose:** Develop Sway in new ways
- **Note:** Winners did not need to be a nonprofit to receive the prize. DW was designated as the recipient at the time of the award.

### What Happened

1. The full $150,000 was transferred to DW's bank account.
2. DW spent approximately **$25,000** on Sway IP development.
3. Nick and Simon subsequently decided that DW should **not** own Sway IP — a separate for-profit entity will develop and own the IP instead.
4. Upon realizing this created a compliance concern, **spending was stopped**.
5. **$125,000 remains unspent** in DW's bank account.

---

## The Compliance Issue

### The Problem

501(c)(3) organizations are prohibited from providing **private benefit** — using charitable assets to enrich private parties without adequate return.

- **The $25K already spent:** DW paid for IP development, but DW will not own the resulting IP. The IP will benefit a for-profit entity controlled by DW's founders. This could be characterized as private benefit.

- **The $125K remaining:** If transferred directly to a for-profit controlled by DW's founders, this would be a clear private benefit/self-dealing issue.

### Mitigating Factors

- The original intent was for DW to develop and own Sway IP — the plan changed after the fact.
- Spending stopped as soon as the compliance concern was identified.
- The $125K has not been improperly used.
- The founders are seeking to address this properly rather than ignore it.

## Options for Resolution

### For the $25,000 Already Spent

1. **For-profit reimburses DW** — Once the for-profit entity exists and has funds, it pays DW $25,000 (fair market value) for the IP development work. This makes DW whole and properly allocates the expense to the entity that benefits.

2. **Assign IP to DW** — The Sway IP developed with these funds is assigned to DW, so DW owns what it paid for. (This conflicts with the decision that DW should not own Sway IP.)

3. **Document as good-faith error** — Acknowledge the issue, document that spending stopped when the concern was identified, and note the remediation steps taken.

### For the $125,000 Remaining

1. **Return to Tools Competition** — Contact the Tools Competition, explain the change in plans, and ask if they want the funds returned or can redirect them to the for-profit entity (or to Nick and Simon personally, since they were the competition winners).

2. **Use for DW's exempt purposes** — The funds remain in DW and are used for civil discourse education activities that DW conducts (not Sway IP development). This is compliant but may not align with the original intent.

3. **Grantor permission to repurpose** — Contact Tools Competition and ask for permission to use the funds differently or redirect them. They may have flexibility.

4. **Contract with for-profit at FMV** — DW contracts with the for-profit for services that further DW's exempt purpose, paying fair market value. This is complex and requires careful structuring to avoid private benefit.

---

## Recommended Path Forward

### Immediate (Before/During 501(c)(3) Application)

- **No action required for the application.** This issue is tangential to whether DW qualifies as a 501(c)(3). The application reflects accurate financials (the $150K as revenue, the $25K as expenses, the $125K as cash).
- **Do not transfer the $125K** to any for-profit entity until proper handling is determined.

### After 501(c)(3) Approval

1. **Consult with Foundation Group** — Present this situation and get their guidance on compliant resolution.

2. **Contact Tools Competition** — Explain the situation and ask about options (return, redirect, or permission to repurpose).

3. **Address the $25K** — Most likely path: once the for-profit exists, have it reimburse DW $25,000 for the development work.

4. **Document everything** — Maintain clear records of the timeline, the decision to stop spending, and the remediation steps.
