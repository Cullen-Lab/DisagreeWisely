## 1. Describe in detail the research project(s) planned

**REVISED ANSWER:**

Our research program investigates how to foster constructive disagreement, reduce polarization, and improve reasoning and communication skills through educational interventions. We focus on understanding the effects of structured cross-perspective dialogue — including dialogue facilitated by AI — on these outcomes. Our research activities fall into three main areas:

**Longitudinal classroom studies.** We will study how students' attitudes, dispositions, and communication skills develop over the course of a semester when they regularly engage in structured, text-based discussions with peers who hold different viewpoints. Using de-identified data from student volunteers at partner universities, we aim to replicate and extend preliminary findings suggesting that structured cross-perspective dialogue can substantially reduce student self-censorship, increase willingness to engage with challenging ideas, and improve measurable argumentation and communication skills. Because students will participate in discussions at regular intervals throughout a semester, we can track the development of these skills over time using pre/post assessments, standardized instruments, and behavioral measures.

**Large-scale controlled experiments on AI-facilitated dialogue.** We are planning empirical studies involving approximately 1,000 American adults recruited via established research platforms (e.g., Prolific Academic). These studies will rigorously test whether AI-facilitated text-based dialogue can reduce polarization and promote mutual understanding between people with opposing ideological views. In controlled experimental designs, we will compare different facilitation approaches — varying, for example, the level of proactive guidance provided by an AI facilitator, the degree of emotional sensitivity in AI responses, and the types of prompts and scaffolding offered. We will examine how the effectiveness of these approaches depends on discussion topic, participant demographics, strength of opinions, and the dynamics of conversations as they unfold.

**Open science and data sharing.** We will make our de-identified conversational datasets publicly available through online repositories (e.g., Open Science Framework, Dataverse) for use by researchers across disciplines — including social psychology, human-computer interaction, conflict studies, political science, and communication. These datasets, which will capture rich information about how people discuss contentious topics, will be valuable far beyond our own research questions. We aim to help define and advance the emerging multidisciplinary field of AI-scaffolded human communication.

Our research will employ a variety of educational discussion tools — not exclusively any single platform — and we will collaborate with researchers at other academic and nonprofit institutions. All research findings will be published in peer-reviewed journals and made publicly available.
