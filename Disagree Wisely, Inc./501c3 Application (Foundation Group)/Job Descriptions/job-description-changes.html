Job Description Revisions

Organization: Disagree Wisely, Inc.

Date: January 23, 2026

Basis: Foundation Group consultation with Ren (501(c)(3) specialist)

Key Guidance from Ren: The nonprofit should separate from IP ownership. The for-profit entity will own Sway; the nonprofit focuses purely on research, education, and program delivery. Job descriptions must be updated to remove any mention of platform/IP development to maintain proper nonprofit structure.

CEO Job Description (Simon Cullen)

Change 1: Program & Research Oversight
Direct research agenda; ensure findings inform platform enhancements.
Direct research agenda; ensure findings advance understanding of constructive disagreement.
Ren specifically flagged "platform enhancements" as problematic. The nonprofit should focus on research outcomes, not informing product development, which is IP work belonging to the for-profit.
Change 2: Partnership Development
Negotiate MOUs and data-sharing agreements that accelerate adoption of Sway.
Negotiate MOUs and data-sharing agreements that expand access to civil discourse education.
Removed Sway-specific language. The nonprofit's mission is advancing civil discourse education broadly, not promoting a specific platform owned by the for-profit.

Qualifications

Since the CEO position is filled by Simon Cullen, changed from generic "Required Qualifications" to "Qualifications" (matching CTOO format) and replaced generic requirements with Simon's actual credentials. This removes "product-development cycles" (IP-related) and reflects that both executive positions are already determined.

CTOO Job Description (Nicholas DiBella)

Change 1: Technology Leadership Duty
Technology leadership - oversee technical design and development of the Sway platform
Technology oversight - identify and evaluate educational technologies for civil discourse; advise on tool and platform adoption to support research and program delivery; monitor innovations in the field.
The original duty involved IP development (building Sway), which belongs to the for-profit. Replaced with a technology oversight role appropriate for a nonprofit: researching, evaluating, and advising on educational technologies rather than developing them. This justifies "Technology" in the CTOO title while keeping the role focused on research and program support.
Change 2: Research & Evaluation
Research & evaluation - design and oversee studies on dialogue effectiveness; analyze platform data; publish results; manage research partnerships.
Research & evaluation - design and oversee studies on dialogue effectiveness and civil discourse pedagogy; publish results; manage research partnerships.
"Analyze platform data" implies working with Sway's proprietary data, which ties the nonprofit to IP work. Replaced with broader civil discourse research focus appropriate for a 501(c)(3).
Change 3: Instructor & User Support
Instructor & user support - gather feedback and iterate features to improve teaching outcomes.
Instructor & user support - support educators using research-based tools and resources to improve teaching outcomes.
"Iterate features" is product development work. The nonprofit role should be supporting educators generally, not developing platform features. Reframed to be tool-agnostic.
Change 4: Qualifications
Software architecture, AI & data-analysis experience.
Software architecture and AI experience are technical qualifications for building products/platforms. This relates to IP development, which belongs to the for-profit. The nonprofit CTOO role focuses on operations, research, and education - not software development.
Change 5: Qualifications (Grant Funding)
Record of peer-reviewed publications and $600k+ in grant funding.
Record of peer-reviewed publications and $1M+ in grant funding.
Updated to reflect current grant funding total, which now exceeds $1 million.
Change 6: Public Representation
Public representation - appear on podcasts, webinars, and workshops; develop demos and technical materials for educators, funders, researchers, and users.
Public representation - appear on podcasts, webinars, and workshops; develop educational and training materials, presentations, and program resources for educators, funders, and researchers.
"Demos" implies product demonstrations (i.e., Sway demos), which is for-profit marketing. "Technical materials" suggests platform documentation. "Users" implies platform users. Replaced with language focused on educational program delivery appropriate for a nonprofit. The CTOO title retains "Technology" because the role involves investigating and overseeing various technologies for civil discourse education - a research/evaluation function distinct from IP development.
