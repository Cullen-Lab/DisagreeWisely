sway.html

~/Documents/GitHub/dibellatron.github.io/sway.html

1. Critical

"Sway is provided by Disagree Wisely, Inc."

Line 34

Current
Sway is provided by Disagree Wisely, Inc. in collaboration with Heterodox Academy.
Recommended
Sway was developed in collaboration with Heterodox Academy.

This directly states DW provides Sway, contradicting the clean separation. The IRS application says DW doesn't develop or provide software. An examiner Googling "Nicholas DiBella" will land here. Reframing as past-tense collaboration removes DW from the picture while staying truthful.

2. Important

"We're working to normalize..."

Line 38

Current
We're working to normalize reasoned conversations between students with opposing perspectives and help transform fraught arguments into opportunities for greater understanding.
Recommended
Sway is designed to normalize reasoned conversations between students with opposing perspectives and help transform fraught arguments into opportunities for greater understanding.

Present-tense "we're working" implies Nick (via DW) is actively building/running Sway. Shifting the subject to "Sway" describes the product's purpose without implying ongoing DW involvement in its operation.

3. Recommended

Consider reframing the page intro

Line 34 — first paragraph overall

Current
With Simon Cullen, I co-created Sway, an educational chat platform that connects students with differing perspectives into one-on-one conversations and uses AI to facilitate better discussions between them. Sway is provided by Disagree Wisely, Inc. in collaboration with Heterodox Academy.
Recommended
With Simon Cullen, I co-created Sway, an educational chat platform that connects students with differing perspectives into one-on-one conversations and uses AI to facilitate better discussions between them. Sway was developed in collaboration with Heterodox Academy.

The first sentence is fine — it describes Nick personally as co-creator. The second sentence is the critical fix (see item above). Together, they position Nick as the creator and HxA as a collaborator, without DW appearing as the provider/operator.
