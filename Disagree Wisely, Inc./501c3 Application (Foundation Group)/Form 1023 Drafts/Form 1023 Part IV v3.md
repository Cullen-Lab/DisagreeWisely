# Disagree Wisely, Inc.
## IRS Form 1023 Part IV - Narrative Description of Activities (Version 3)

## Mission and Charitable Purpose

We teach people how to disagree productively. Disagree Wisely, Inc. operates exclusively for charitable and educational purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code. Our mission is to advance education by developing essential intellectual virtues—critical thinking, cognitive empathy, intellectual humility, and constructive disagreement skills—among college students and the broader public.

Our primary charitable purpose addresses a critical gap in higher education: the widespread self-censorship and inability to engage constructively across ideological differences that undermines democratic education and civic preparation.

## Current and Planned Activities

### Activity #1 – Operating the Sway Educational Dialogue Platform

**What:** Provide free, cloud-based chat platform that connects students with opposing viewpoints and facilitates AI-scaffolded educational discussions on contentious topics.

**Who:** 3 software engineers under technical leadership; overseen by Board of Directors.

**Where:** Servers hosted on Google Kubernetes Engine (Iowa and South Carolina zones); accessible to users worldwide.

**When:** Began October 2024; continuous operation.

**Why (exempt purpose):** Advances education by teaching critical thinking, empathy, and constructive dialogue skills through real-time AI guidance and feedback during peer discussions.

**% of time/resources (FY 2025):** 55% of staff hours; 48% of budget (hosting, development, AI processing costs).

**Funding:** Foundation grants; beginning 2026, program-service fees from institutions priced to recover direct and allocable indirect costs only (no profit margin).

### Activity #2 – Educator Training & Support

**What:** Provide technical support, training workshops, and pedagogical resources for instructors implementing dialogue-based teaching methods.

**Who:** Co-founders and part-time assistant.

**Where:** Online training sessions; in-person workshops at academic conferences.

**When:** Ongoing since Fall 2024; expanding Summer 2025.

**Why (exempt purpose):** Advances education by training faculty in evidence-based methods for facilitating constructive disagreement and civil discourse.

**% of time/resources (FY 2025):** 10% of staff hours; 7% of budget.

**Funding:** Foundation grants.

### Activity #3 – Applied Research & Publication

**What:** Conduct controlled studies on dialogue effectiveness; publish findings in peer-reviewed journals; collaborate with academic researchers.

**Who:** Co-founders with university research partners.

**Where:** Multiple university campuses; results published in academic journals.

**When:** Ongoing since 2024; planned expansion 2025-2027.

**Why (exempt purpose):** Advances education by generating and disseminating scientific knowledge about effective dialogue pedagogy and polarization reduction.

**% of time/resources (FY 2025):** 15% of staff hours; 10% of budget.

**Funding:** Foundation grants; university partnerships.

**Note:** All human-subjects research is or will be IRB-approved.

## Compliance and Risk Management

### Intellectual Property

Disagree Wisely, Inc. owns all Sway intellectual property. Any licenses to third parties will be non-exclusive and at fair-market royalty rates, with proceeds used solely for exempt purposes. No insiders receive royalties or IP income.

### Data Privacy and Educational Compliance

We maintain FERPA-compliant data handling procedures for all student information. All data is encrypted and anonymized for research purposes. Privacy policies comply with applicable state and federal education privacy laws.

### Political Activity and Lobbying

Disagree Wisely, Inc. engages in **zero** political campaign intervention. No more than an insubstantial part (<5%) of our activities will constitute lobbying. Our educational approach is strictly nonpartisan, focusing on developing dialogue skills rather than promoting political positions.

### International Activities

While students in more than a dozen countries access our educational platform, no funds are transferred to foreign governments or sanctioned entities. All activities are limited to education and research.

### Commercial Activities and UBIT

Premium institutional subscriptions beginning in 2026 will only recover costs of mission-related educational services. No unrelated business income is anticipated. Any incidental unrelated business income will be reported on Form 990-T.
