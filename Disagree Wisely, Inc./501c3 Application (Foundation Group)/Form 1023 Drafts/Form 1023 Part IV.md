# Disagree Wisely, Inc.
## IRS Form 1023 Part IV - Narrative Description of Activities

## Mission and Charitable Purpose

### Our Mission
We aim to empower millions of learners to cultivate essential intellectual virtues—humility, cognitive empathy, critical thinking, and the ability to engage productively across differences. By fostering free expression and robust debate, we empower individuals to think independently and contribute constructively to democratic discourse.

### Charitable Purpose and Legal Foundation
Disagree Wisely, Inc. operates exclusively for charitable and educational purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code. Our organization addresses a critical gap in higher education by providing students with tools and opportunities to develop essential intellectual virtues including critical thinking, cognitive empathy, intellectual humility, and the ability to engage in constructive disagreement across ideological differences.

Our primary charitable purpose is to advance education by creating and maintaining Sway, an AI-scaffolded chat platform that facilitates constructive dialogue between college students with differing perspectives on contentious issues. We provide this platform free of charge to students and educational institutions, serving the public good by fostering civil discourse, reducing polarization, and preparing students for democratic citizenship.

## Educational Problem Being Addressed

National surveys reveal widespread self-censorship on American college campuses, with many students avoiding important discussions due to fear of social and academic repercussions. This phenomenon undermines students' ability to critically examine their values, understand opposing viewpoints, and participate effectively in civil discourse—skills essential for both academic achievement and democratic citizenship.

Current educational approaches often fail to provide students with safe, structured environments where they can practice engaging with those who hold fundamentally different views. This deficit leaves graduates unprepared for the intellectual and civic challenges they will face in their professional and personal lives, contributing to broader societal polarization and democratic dysfunction.

## Our Educational Solution: The Sway Platform

### Platform Overview
Sway is a chat platform similar to iMessage or WhatsApp that connects students who disagree about contentious issues and facilitates better discussions between them. The platform employs two key educational scaffolding mechanisms:

**AI Facilitation:** An AI Guide participates in every conversation, functioning like an experienced teacher by posing insightful questions, clarifying vague arguments, providing relevant factual information, de-escalating tense moments, and ensuring students engage meaningfully rather than talking past each other.

**Smart Rephrasing:** When students compose messages containing inflammatory or unconstructive language, Sway suggests better ways to express their ideas, preserving core meaning while providing immediate feedback to help students develop habits of clear and respectful communication.

### Educational Methodology
Our approach is grounded in established educational principles and empirically validated through controlled research studies. By providing real-time scaffolding and feedback, Sway creates learning opportunities that would be difficult or impossible to replicate in traditional classroom settings, particularly for sensitive or polarizing topics.

## Track Record and Achievements

Our early experimental studies in 2024 demonstrated Sway's educational effectiveness through rigorous scientific methodology. In controlled studies with 120 participants discussing topics including abortion, Israel-Palestine, and immigration, participants showed significant improvements in their ability to understand and respect opposing perspectives—improving by approximately 0.4 standard deviations (p < 0.0001; 95% CI: 0.24 to 0.59). This represents a substantial educational impact by academic research standards.

### Classroom Integration and Student Outcomes
In Fall 2024, Sway was integrated into a large UCLA course serving approximately 250 students. The platform facilitated numerous conversations between students with opposing viewpoints on gender-related topics. Students overwhelmingly reported that Sway enhanced both the quality of their conversations and their foundation of mutual respect and critical thinking skills, as documented in our publicly available student feedback data.

### Typical Classroom Deployment: UCLA Sociology of Gender Course (Fall 2024)

**Who:** 250 students enrolled in undergraduate sociology course

**What:** Each student participated in several (between 4-6) 35-minute Sway discussion assignments throughout the semester. The discussion topics, set by the instructor, covered issues including educational disadvantages faced by boys/men, religious freedom and hijab laws, and gender in electoral politics

**Results:** 94% of students rated conversations as "Good" or "Awesome"; 97% reported their partner was respectful; only 0.3% of messages flagged as potentially unconstructive
