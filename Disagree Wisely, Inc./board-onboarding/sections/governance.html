Governance Policies

▾

The board has adopted the governance policies required by our Bylaws (Article XI) and expected by the IRS for 501(c)(3) organizations. Below is a summary of each; expand any subsection for details.

Bylaws

▾

Adopted July 29, 2025. Signed by Cullen, DiBella, and Thomason.

The Bylaws are the foundational governing document. Key provisions:

  • PurposeExclusively charitable, consistent with IRC § 501(c)(3).
  • MembersNo governing members beyond the Board itself.
  • Board sizeMinimum 3, maximum 15 directors.
  • Terms3 years, staggered.
  • QuorumMajority of all directors (currently 3 of 5).
  • RemovalBy majority vote of the other directors, with written notice and opportunity to be heard.
  • OfficersPresident, Vice President(s), Secretary, Treasurer. Elected annually from among directors. One person may hold multiple offices except President and Secretary must be different.
  • CommitteesThe board may create committees with delegated powers. Advisory boards may include non-directors but cannot bind the corporation.
  • Email consentUnanimous written consent (including by email) is valid for any board action (§ 4.7, § 8.4).
  • IndemnificationComprehensive protection for directors acting in good faith. The corporation advances defense expenses. See the Insurance section for D&O coverage.
  • AmendmentsBylaws amended by majority vote of all directors in office at a duly convened meeting, with notice including a copy of the proposed amendment.
  • Fiscal yearJanuary 1 – December 31.

Conflict of Interest Policy

▾

Adopted July 29, 2025. Signed by Cullen, DiBella, and Thomason.

Action required: Every director must sign the annual Conflict of Interest Acknowledgment upon joining the board and each year thereafter. See the Action Items at the top of this page.

This policy is especially important because two directors (Simon and Nick) also serve as paid officers. Key provisions:

  • Disclosure duty: If you have a direct or indirect financial interest in any transaction the organization is considering, you must disclose all material facts to the board.
  • Determining a conflict: After your disclosure, you leave the room (or call). The remaining directors decide whether a conflict exists.
  • Recusal: If a conflict is found, you may present information but must then leave while the disinterested directors deliberate and vote.
  • Compensation votes: A board member who receives compensation from the organization may not vote on matters pertaining to their own compensation. They may provide information to a compensation committee.
  • Board composition: The majority of the board must be non-salaried and not related to salaried personnel.
  • Annual acknowledgment: Each director signs a statement annually confirming they have received, read, and agree to comply with the policy.
  • Periodic reviews: The board must periodically review whether compensation arrangements are reasonable, using appropriate comparability data.

Financial Authorization Resolution

▾

Originally adopted September 28, 2025. Amended December 13, 2025.

This is the operative document governing how the organization spends money. It defines what the President and Treasurer can approve on their own, and what requires full board approval.

Items Requiring Board Approval

  • Any single commitment over $25,000 or multi-year obligation over $50,000 total
  • New debt, credit lines, or capital purchases over $10,000
  • Grants to third parties over $15,000 per recipient per year
  • New executive employees (officers, C-suite, key employees under Form 990)
  • Related-party transactions or anything presenting a conflict of interest

Personnel Hiring (Dec 2025 Amendment)

The President and Treasurer, acting jointly, may hire non-executive employees within the approved budget without board approval, provided employment terms are standard. They must report all new hires at the next board meeting.

Emergency Authority

  • Solo officer: up to $10,000
  • Joint (President + Treasurer): up to $25,000
  • Requires board notification within 24 hours and written memo within 48 hours

Board Oversight

Each quarter, the board reviews the Treasurer's statement of activity and spot-checks up to 3 randomly selected transactions over $1,000. Takes about 5 minutes.

Document Retention Policy

▾

Adopted November 16, 2025 by email vote.

Establishes how long the organization keeps different types of records, and what to do if litigation or an audit is anticipated.

Key Retention Periods

Record TypeRetention
Articles of Incorporation, bylaws, minutes, resolutions, tax returns, annual financial statementsPermanent
Bank/card statements, invoices, receipts, payroll records, grant records, contracts7 years
Conflict of interest disclosures7 years
Unsuccessful grant proposals3 years

Roles

  • Secretary (Nick DiBella): Maintains corporate governance records — articles, bylaws, minutes, resolutions, policies.
  • Treasurer (Nick DiBella): Maintains financial and accounting records — bank statements, payroll, tax filings, grant financials.
Litigation Hold: If the organization becomes aware of actual or anticipated litigation, government investigation, or audit, all routine destruction of potentially related records must immediately stop until counsel or the board authorizes resumption.

Anti-Harassment and Discrimination Policy

▾

Adopted August 2025.

Applies to all employees, officers, directors, contractors, and volunteers in all work-related settings (virtual or in-person).

  • Protected characteristics: Race, color, ethnicity, national origin, religion, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation, age, disability, pregnancy, veteran status, and any other legally protected characteristic.
  • Reporting: Contact the President — Simon Cullen at simon@disagreewisely.org.
  • No retaliation: Retaliation against good-faith reporters is prohibited.
  • Investigation: Complaints are acknowledged promptly, investigated fairly, and kept confidential to the extent possible.
