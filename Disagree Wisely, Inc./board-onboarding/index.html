About Disagree Wisely

▾

Disagree Wisely, Inc. is a Florida nonprofit corporation incorporated on April 2, 2025. We are a civil discourse research and education organization: we investigate how people can learn to reason together across genuine differences, and we develop and deliver evidence-based educational programs grounded in that research.

The organization grew out of work at Carnegie Mellon University, where co-founders Simon Cullen and Nick DiBella developed educational tools — including Sway, an AI-scaffolded dialogue platform — for structured cross-perspective discussion in university classrooms. Disagree Wisely was originally incorporated with the intent of housing both the Sway platform and the surrounding research program. After extensive consultation with Foundation Group (our 501(c)(3) advisors), the organization restructured in early 2026 to draw a clean separation: Sway’s intellectual property remains with the co-founders, who plan to establish a separate for-profit entity to hold it, while Disagree Wisely focuses exclusively on research, education, and program delivery — using a variety of educational tools in its work.

Mission Statement

We aim to empower millions of learners to cultivate essential intellectual virtues — humility, cognitive empathy, critical thinking, and the ability to engage productively across differences. Driven by rigorous empirical research, we deploy and study innovative educational tools that foster free inquiry and intelligent discussion.

Traction

  • 2,000+ student users across the co-founders’ educational programs to date
  • 200+ instructors across 150+ institutions signed up
  • 20,000+ students expected in the 2025–2026 academic year
  • Over $700,000 raised from prizes, grants, and charitable contributions

Learn more at disagreewisely.org.

501(c)(3) Status

▾

Our 501(c)(3) application is pending. We are working with Foundation Group (our nonprofit consulting firm) to prepare and file IRS Form 1023. Key points:

  • We have a 27-month filing window from incorporation (through approximately July 2027) for retroactive tax-exempt status.
  • A federal FIPSE grant (~$800K for research on educational tools for civil discourse) is contingent on receiving 501(c)(3) determination, creating real urgency.
  • The application frames Disagree Wisely as a civil discourse research and education organization that studies constructive disagreement and delivers evidence-based educational programs. Per Foundation Group’s advice, the application focuses on Disagree Wisely’s research and educational mission broadly — not on any specific tool or platform.
IP structure: Sway platform IP is personally owned by Simon Cullen and Nick DiBella — not by Disagree Wisely. The co-founders plan to establish a separate for-profit entity to hold the IP and fund product development. This separation was a key recommendation from Foundation Group to avoid private benefit concerns with the IRS. Disagree Wisely’s nonprofit activities — research, education, program delivery — are structurally independent of Sway’s commercial development.

Conflict of Interest Policy

▾

Adopted July 29, 2025. Signed by Cullen, DiBella, and Thomason.

Annual attestation: Per Foundation Group, the board must attest to understanding this policy annually, and that attestation should be recorded in meeting minutes. The IRS looks for this if they audit. Individual signed acknowledgment forms are not required for the IRS application, but the board may choose to use them as a best practice.

This policy is especially important because two directors (Simon and Nick) also serve as paid officers. Key provisions:

  • Disclosure duty: If you have a direct or indirect financial interest in any transaction the organization is considering, you must disclose all material facts to the board.
  • Determining a conflict: After your disclosure, you leave the room (or call). The remaining directors decide whether a conflict exists.
  • Recusal: If a conflict is found, you may present information but must then leave while the disinterested directors deliberate and vote.
  • Compensation votes: A board member who receives compensation from the organization may not vote on matters pertaining to their own compensation. They may provide information to a compensation committee.
  • Board composition: The majority of the board must be non-salaried and not related to salaried personnel.
  • Annual attestation: The board attests to understanding this policy annually, recorded in meeting minutes.
  • Periodic reviews: The board must periodically review whether compensation arrangements are reasonable, using appropriate comparability data.

Insurance Coverage

▾

All directors are covered by comprehensive insurance arranged through Gallagher (Arthur J. Gallagher Risk Management Services). Both policies are claims-made with full prior acts coverage, meaning they cover claims arising from wrongful acts going back to the beginning of the organization’s operations (June 2024) — not just from the policy start date. Both are active through November 14, 2026. Total annual premium: $4,600.

Technology / Cyber Liability Insurance

▾
  • InsurerCFC Underwriting (Lloyd’s of London)
  • Policy #ESO0040579703
  • Coverage$1,000,000 aggregate limit
  • Deductible$2,500 each and every claim (including costs and expenses)
  • Defense costsInside the limit — defense costs and expenses erode the $1M coverage
  • ClassificationAI Software Developer
  • ScopeWorldwide
  • Premium$3,780/year
  • PeriodNovember 14, 2025 – November 14, 2026 (12 months)
  • RetroactiveFull prior acts

Covers technology errors and omissions, data breaches, cyber incidents, and reputational harm. Independent contractors performing technology services are covered as “employees” under the policy. Given that Disagree Wisely deploys educational technology tools to thousands of students across many institutions and handles sensitive student data in its research programs, cyber and professional liability coverage is essential. 24-hour cyber incident hotline: 1-844-677-4155.

Classification note: This policy was purchased in November 2025, before the January 2026 restructuring. It carries an “AI Software Developer” classification that reflects the pre-restructuring scope. At renewal (November 2026), the classification should be updated with Gallagher to reflect DW’s actual role as a research and education organization that deploys third-party tools — not a software developer.
Defense costs distinction: The D&O policy pays defense costs in addition to the $1M limit (so legal fees don’t reduce what’s available for damages). The Cyber/E&O policy includes defense costs within the $1M limit (legal fees reduce available coverage). This means that in a cyber or E&O claim, expensive litigation could consume a significant portion of the coverage before any settlement or judgment is paid.

Bylaws Indemnification

▾

Beyond insurance, the Bylaws (Article IX) provide:

  • No personal liability for monetary damages unless the director engaged in self-dealing, willful misconduct, or recklessness.
  • Indemnification against expenses, judgments, fines, and settlements for directors acting in good faith.
  • Advancement of defense expenses prior to final disposition (conditioned on repayment if not ultimately entitled).
  • Rights continue after leaving the board and extend to heirs.
